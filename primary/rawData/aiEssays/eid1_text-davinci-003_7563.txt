

The ending of Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho is meant to represent hope and resilience in the face of difficulty. The winter hibiscus plant in the story is a symbol of this. Through the winter, the protagonist's family and community struggles against all odds. However, despite the hardships they face, the protagonist finds solace in the knowledge that the winter hibiscus will return in the spring and the tests of life can be taken again. This message of hope is meant to provide comfort and courage to the reader. By ending with the protagonist's wish to take the test again when the winter hibiscus blooms, the author emphasizes the idea that no matter how difficult a situation is, it can be overcome.
