

The winter hibiscus in the poem symbolizes the narrator's struggle to gain the necessary qualifications for success. By ending the poem with a reminder of the winter hibiscus returning in the spring, the narrator is expressing hope that they will be successful in their endeavors, symbolizing another opportunity to take the test they need in order to achieve success. The imagery of the melting snow and geese returning associated with the winter hibiscus budding again provides a striking image of rebirth and renewal, painting a confident picture of success and possibilities for the future.
