

At the end of Winter Hibiscus, the narrator reflects on the difficulty of growing a hibiscus plant in the winter. They feel disheartened that the plant won't bloom until the spring, but find hope in the knowledge that with patience and resilience, they will eventually succeed. By ending with the expectation of taking the test again when the snows melt and the geese return and the hibiscus blooms, the narrator's sentiment of hope and determination is conveyed. It also underscores the idea that life can be challenging, but with dedication and perseverance, it is possible to triumph in the end.
