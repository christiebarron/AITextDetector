

The protagonist in the story, a girl named Ah Xian, wants to take an English language proficiency test. Ah Xian's grandmother tells her the story of the winter hibiscus. The winter hibiscus is a flower that does not bloom in winter, but will return again in the spring when the geese return and the snow melts.

The grandmother's story is meant to teach Ah Xian about patience and perseverance, which she will need if she is to succeed on the English language proficiency test. The story ends with the grandmother reminding Ah Xian to wait patiently until the winter hibiscus is in bloom and for the geese to return, signaling that spring has arrived. She is then to take the test again. The ending of the story serves as a reminder for the protagonist and for the readers to be patient, be hopeful, and don't give up.
