

The ending of Winter Hibiscus is intended to be hopeful. The narrator has just recalled a memory of when their family had to take a tough and intimidating test. The narrator expresses anticipation that it will be different and easier the next time the family has to take the test, when they return in the spring. The image of the hibiscus blooming again symbolizes the promise of a new beginning and the potential for a better outcome the next time the test is taken. This image of hope provides closure to the story.
