

The ending of Minfong Ho's poem Winter Hibiscus emphasizes the passage of time and the cycle of nature that exists in the world. The poem follows a narrator as they experience the feelings of anxiety and dread that come with the expectation of a test, only to find peace in the beauty and resilience of the winter hibiscus. By describing the eventual blooming of the flower in the spring, the narrator finds hope in the knowledge that their own fears will eventually pass, and they will have the opportunity to try again. The ending serves to remind us that time passes, and that we can all take comfort in knowing that even our darkest moments will eventually give way to new beginnings.
