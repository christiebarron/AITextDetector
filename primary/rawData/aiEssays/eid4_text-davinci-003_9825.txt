

The ending of Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho is meant to suggest that spring is a time of growth, renewal, and new beginnings. The protagonist looks back to the winter and the fear and uncertainty he experienced during the darkness of that season. He sees the hope of spring as a way to succeed again and make up for his earlier short-comings. By ending with the hope for a second chance through a better result on the test, the story carries with it an uplifting message about the possibilities of the future.
