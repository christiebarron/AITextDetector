

At the beginning of Winter Hibiscus, the main character is struggling with a difficult math test. She knows the material but is too anxious to perform on the test. By the end of the story, the main character has seen a winter hibiscus blooming and it symbolizes hope and renewal. With this newfound optimism, she mentions that she will take the test again when they come back in the spring. This ends the story on a hopeful note, suggesting that the main character will succeed in her test if she takes it when the flowers bloom because she will now be feeling more confident and optimistic.
