

The story of Winter Hibiscus is based on a young girl named Alu who has immigrated into America in order to make a better life for her family. Alu has been struggling to adjust to her new life in America and is having difficulty passing a vital English test. At the end of the story, Alu finds hope in the hibiscus flower blooming through the snow. By comparing the hibiscus' persistence and resiliency to her own, Alu finds solace and is inspired with renewed strength and hope for the future. The ending of the story is meant to be symbolic of Alu's own resilience and determination to pass her English test. The line asks the reader to imagine a hopeful future in which Alu has persevered despite the difficult challenge of adjusting to a new life in a foreign country and has been successful in passing her English test.
