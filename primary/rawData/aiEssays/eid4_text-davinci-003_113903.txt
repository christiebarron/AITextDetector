

The ending of Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho serves as a symbol of perseverance for the protagonist, and in a way, for the reader as well. The protagonist of the story is an immigrant student in America who struggles with taking the English proficiency test. In the end of the story, it is the return of the winter hibiscus that symbolizes the protagonist's determination to try again to pass the English test despite her struggles. By ending with a hopeful thought, the author is conveying the idea that the protagonist will eventually succeed if she tries hard enough and never gives up. This hopeful thought also creates a more uplifting tone for the reader and encourages them to keep trying in their process of learning.
