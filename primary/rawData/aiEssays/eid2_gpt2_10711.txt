That's not the only problem in the U.S. with library censorship. The library is now required by the U.S. Constitution to turn over books that are of "appropriate educational significance; and for each book removed they shall receive one free copy of that book, unless those books are for educational purposes."

The problem, as it turned out, wasn't so that the U.S. Library of Congress didn't remove books. First Amendment jurisprudence dictates that "Congress may make no law respecting an establishment of religion, or prohibiting the free exercise thereof."

The federal government doesn't like that. Last year, the Library of Congress passed a resolution calling it unconstitutional to deny visitors the ability to buy books from Amazon.
