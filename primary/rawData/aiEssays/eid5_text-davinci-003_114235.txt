

The memoir “From Home: The Blueprints of Our Lives” by Narciso Rodriguez paints a vivid picture of the author’s life growing up in an immigrant family in New Jersey. Through the use of vivid imagery and evocative language, Narciso creates a strong sense of nostalgia and longing in the memoir. He expertly conveys the feeling of being in between two cultures—between his Dominican upbringing and the American culture that surrounds him.

Narciso paints a vivid picture of the small New Jersey apartment in which he grew up, with its cramped yet warm rooms filled with family. He shares memories of his childhood, and the happiness, family gatherings, music, laughter, and sense of security this home had always provided him. Through his words readers can experience his longing for that same sense of security that his home had once provided.

The memoir often reflects on childhood innocence, as Narciso recalls memories of his childhood dreams of becoming a fashion designer and the cultural divide between two nations. He talks of his passion for fashion and how it connected him to his Dominican heritage and uplifted him in a world where he felt pressure to conform to American culture. Through this, he shows readers the beauty of dual identities within a single individual.
