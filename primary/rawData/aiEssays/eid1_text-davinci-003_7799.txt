

Narciso Rodriguez's memoir 'From Home: The Blueprints of Our Lives' is a powerful exploration of his identity and the memories that have shaped it. Even as he shares his childhood and upbringing, Rodriguez creates a mood of nostalgia, replete with colors, tastes, and emotions. 

The memoir opens with a vivid description of Rodriguez's return to his hometown of Paterson, New Jersey, and his subsequent exploration of the surrounding areas. The reader is immediately immersed in a time and place that is keenly felt and deeply personal. Rodriguez effectively captures the sensory details of the town, invoking the smell of the bakery, the sounds of neighbors gossiping, and the anticipation of his mother's home cooking. By the end of the first chapter, the reader is entrenched in a world defined by warmth and love.

Through the following chapters, Rodriguez's mood of nostalgia is reinforced by his observations of the changes to his hometown and his own recognition of life's passing. He balances the sweet memories of childhood with the harsh realities of adulthood. This is seen in his recounting of his grandmother’s death and his reflections on his journey from hometown to global citizen.
