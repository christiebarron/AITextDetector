

The ending of Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho conveys a sense of hope and resilience. The hibiscus flower is a metaphor for the protagonist's journey of overcoming personal obstacles and hardship. The last line implies that, despite the difficult times, the protagonist is optimistic that they will find the strength to take the test again when spring returns. In a way, it serves to remind the reader that even in the darkest of times, we can find hope and take solace in the idea that better times are just around the corner.
