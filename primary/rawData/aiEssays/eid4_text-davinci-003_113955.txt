

In this ending passage, Minfong Ho is expressing the idea that things will come full circle. He talks of when "the snows melt and the geese return and this hibiscus is budding." To him, this symbolizes a cycle of renewal and hope. Just as the hibiscus will blossom and the snow will melt in the spring, he believes that he will be able to face his doubts and take the test again. This expresses his belief that things will work out for the better in the end.
