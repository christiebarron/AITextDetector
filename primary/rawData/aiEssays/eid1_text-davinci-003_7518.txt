

Minfong Ho ends Winter Hibiscus with this line to illustrate the narrator's determination to make another effort to pass the test. The narrator is comparing himself to the hibiscus flower, which will also have to come back in the spring in order to bloom. The imagery of snow melting and geese returning expresses a new beginning, implying that the narrator will have a chance to start over and try again. The ending of the story serves as a reminder to readers that hope and perseverance can get someone through difficult times.
