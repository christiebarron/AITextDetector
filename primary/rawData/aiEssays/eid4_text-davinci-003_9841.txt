

The ending of Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho signifies hope. The narrator's reference to the hibiscus and the geese returning symbolically reflect the idea of resilience despite the challenges of winter. The narrator expresses the feeling that despite facing difficulties, the spring will bring new hope. Additionally, the narrator suggests that he will take the test again in the spring to signify perseverance and confidence. Despite the difficult winter season, the narrator will work hard to achieve success in the future. The symbols associated with the hibiscus flower and the geese further emphasize the narrator's resilient attitude.
