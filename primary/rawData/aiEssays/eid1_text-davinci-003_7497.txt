

Minfong Ho ends her poem "Winter Hibiscus" with this line to emphasize the idea of renewal and hope. Just as these lines hint at, spring brings new growth and the opportunity to try again. The poem, which contemplates failing a difficult test, concludes by suggesting that the speaker will try again in the future, when the flower of the title begins to bloom. This line implies that the speaker will come out of this difficult experience a stronger and wiser person, able to succeed in their goals. In this way, the poem's ending serves as a reminder that even in difficult moments, there can still be hope and a chance to start anew.
