

The ending of Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho ties directly into the protagonist's story arc of growth. The character is a young woman who is growing up and faced with a variety of decisions. Throughout the story the protagonist is faced with a number of tests and decisions that are difficult and hard to make. The protagonist chooses to wait until the following spring before taking an important test that she has been struggling with. This ending serves to show how the protagonist has taken the time to reflect and mature, and will no longer be rushing into decisions. It also highlights the idea that life is cyclical and that her current struggles will pass, but that these tests will still be there when she is ready to take them.
