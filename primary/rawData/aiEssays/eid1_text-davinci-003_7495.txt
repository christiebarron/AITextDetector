

The narrator of Winter Hibiscus, a young girl, is preparing to take a language proficiency test, but becomes overwhelmed and anxious because of her fear of failure. She then turns to the hibiscus tree behind her house for solace. The narrator recognizes the resilience of the hibiscus, which remains through the winter despite the cold. By ending the story with the hibiscus in mind, the narrator is reminded of the lasting power of nature, her own potential, and her ability to achieve success. It is a representation of the narrator's hope that she will be able to overcome her fear and persevere like the hibiscus tree, and ultimately achieve her greatest potential. This provides a reassuring and hopeful closure for the story.
