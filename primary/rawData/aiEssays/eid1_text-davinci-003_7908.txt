

'From Home: The Blueprints of Our Lives' by Narciso Rodriguez is a memoir filled with thought-provoking stories about Rodriguez's life. Throughout the memoir, Rodriguez expresses a range of emotion that creates a strong sense of nostalgia and longing. 

In the book, Rodriguez recalls the memories of his youth growing up in a Cuban-American family in New Jersey. He delves into the connections and differences between his childhood in New York and his adolescence in Florida. His honest descriptions of his relationships with family, friends, and even strangers evoke a feeling of solitude and nostalgia. 

The readers of the memoir are given a glimpse into Rodriguez's often tumultuous and sometimes blissful youth. Although often contrasting in tone, the feeling of Rodriguez's childhood and adolescence is one of profound joy and longing. Through his introspection and vivid memories, Rodriguez creates an atmosphere of longing and yearning. 

Rodriguez also takes the time to explore his place within the world, focusing on themes of identity, belonging, and the idea of home. His detailed explorations of how race, culture, and sexuality impact identity and his place in the world further emphasize the overall feeling of longing in the memoir.
