

In Joe Kurmaski’s “Rough Road Ahead,” the setting and its features directly contribute to the cyclist’s experience throughout the story. The road features potholes that have been filled with water, making them extremely difficult to navigate without damaging the bike and adversely affecting the cyclist’s progress. The terrain is especially treacherous considering that it is unpaved and the cyclist has no visibility beyond the headlight of the bike. This would make it more challenging to spot potential obstacles in the cyclist’s path until it is already too late.

The darkness of night that is presented in the story also serves to heighten the cyclist’s precarious position. Without sufficient light, it is likely that the cyclist couldn’t accurately gauge the depth of the potholes, nor can he be sure of what is in them or of how far they may go in any given direction. Furthermore, he is unable to anticipate how quickly the terrain is going to shift. As a result, it is difficult for the cyclist to accurately calculate how much effort he can put into each stroke in order to continue making progress.
