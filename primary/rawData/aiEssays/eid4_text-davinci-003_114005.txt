

This ending emphasizes the idea of rebirth in nature. In the poem, the speaker is faced with an upcoming test they must take and passes it. However, the speaker feels largely uncertain, so they look to the surrounding natural world for guidance and resolve. The imagery of the hibiscus being able to bud again come springtime, much like themselves, implies a hope for continued growth and perseverance throughout coming struggles. The speaker finds solace in the idea of the hibiscus blooming in the spring and resolves to take the test again when that time comes, implying that they will be better prepared and more successful.
