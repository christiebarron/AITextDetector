

The narrator of this poem is a young girl who is taking a standardized test in her school. She is reflecting on the winter and looking forward to the spring. She wonders what the changes will bring when the winter ends and spring returns, and when the snows melt and the geese come back. The narrator has been feeling discouraged and overwhelmed by the test, but she believes that her perspective will change when the season does, and she will be able to re-take the test with renewed optimism and a different outlook. The ending of the poem is meant to convey the narrator's hope that she will be able to move forward and make progress when the season changes.
