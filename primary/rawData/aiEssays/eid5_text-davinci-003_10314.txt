

Narciso Rodriguez's memoir 'From Home: The Blueprints of Our Lives' is a powerful and captivating story that paints a vivid picture of the struggles and triumphs of his immigrant family and his own experiences as a creative individual. The memoir takes the reader on an emotional journey through Rodriguez’s life, as he seeks to understand himself and his place in the world.

Throughout the memoir, Rodriguez creates a mood of nostalgia, sorrow, excitement, joy, and awe. Through the use of vivid sensory details and intimate descriptions of his family’s experiences, Rodriguez beautifully captures the emotions of the time. Rodriguez quickly establishes his connection to his homeland of Puerto Rico and the accompanying sentimentality of home. The memoir conveys Rodriguez’s journey through immigration to America, and his difficulties adapting to life in a new country. The story provides memorable scenes that bring forth feelings of joy and admiration for his family’s strength.

In addition, Rodriguez paints a portrait of youthful optimism and joy. He recounts his childhood memories of sports and dance, as well as the excitement he felt pursuing his dreams of becoming a fashion designer. The tone of the memoir is hopeful and driven, and Rodriguez’s admiration for his family is clear.
