

Narciso Rodriguez's memoir, 'From Home: The Blueprints of Our Lives', is written with an intense sense of emotion that resonates throughout the pages of the book. From the opening lines, the mood is one of melancholia and reflection, as Rodriguez shares his recollections of his childhood in New Jersey. Through reminiscences of his parents' struggles, of his own extraordinary achievements, and of his strong ties to his family and culture, Rodriguez creates a mood of bittersweet nostalgia that permeates the work. Rodriguez's writing is vivid and evocative, and his descriptions serve to paint a powerful picture of his life story. 

The reader is immediately immersed in a world of strong familial bonds and intangible ties that have formed the basis of Rodriguez's life. There is an air of tenderness and reverence as he speaks of his upbringing and memories with fondness and love. From describing teaching moments with his parents to memories of the orange blossom scent in a garden, Rodriguez has a deep understanding of the energy, grace, and fragility of these precious memories. 

Thus, the mood created in the memoir 'From Home: The Blueprints of Our Lives' by Narciso Rodriguez is one of wistful reflection, of revisiting experiences with shared nostalgia, of moments of deep connection, and of moments that gave rise to lifetime bonds.
