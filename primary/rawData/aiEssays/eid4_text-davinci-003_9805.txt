

The ending of Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho serves as a reminder to the protagonist that although she was initially unsuccessful in her efforts to pass her test, she will have the opportunity to try again in the future. By ending this way, the author emphasizes the protagonist's determination and strength of character, reinforcing the idea that even when we face adversity, it is important to keep our heads held high and strive for better. Additionally, the vivid imagery of nature and the passing of the seasons paints a hopeful picture of renewal and growth, suggesting that with effort and dedication, things can, and will, change for the better.
