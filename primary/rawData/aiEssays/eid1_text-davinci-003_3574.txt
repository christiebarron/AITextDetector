

The newsworthy debate over censorship in libraries has long been one of the most controversial topics in our society. As the number of people using libraries for research, entertainment, and education continues to increase, the challenge of censorship grows. Libraries are important public institutions that need to ensure safety, promote responsible use of their collection, and provide access to information.

The question is not whether or not certain materials, like books, music, movies, and magazines, should be censored and removed from library shelves if they are found to be offensive. The question is how they should be censored in a fair and ethical manner. The library should offer materials that reflect diverse perspectives, including those with which we may not agree. It should be understood that such works may be considered controversial, but library users should always be allowed to make their own decisions about what they read, watch, and listen to. 

Libraries have a responsibility to the public to provide information that is accurate and unbiased. This means that it is important for library workers to be aware of the current political, social, and cultural climate, and be able to distinguish opinion from fact.
