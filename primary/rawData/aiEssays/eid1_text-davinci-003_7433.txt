

The ending of Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho serves as a reminder of the protagonist's hope for the future despite the challenges she faced in the winter. The protagonist wanted to take a difficult school exam, but was too overwhelmed that winter. The return of spring, the melting of snow, and the return of geese all serve as natural symbols of hope and renewal. Thus, by ending the story with the protagonist's determination to retake the exam, the author conveys the idea that despite any hardships the protagonist may face, she will continue to try in the future.
