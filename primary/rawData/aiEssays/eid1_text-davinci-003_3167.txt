

Dear Editor, 

I am writing to express my opinion on the effects of computers on people. Technology has become ubiquitous in our lives, and computers are now integral parts of the way people work, study, and communicate. While these advances have undoubtedly opened up a great deal of possibility and convenience, there are also a few downsides which can’t be overlooked. 

For one, technology can be isolating. When people communicate via text or email rather than face-to-face, social interaction can suffer. People can also become addicted to spending time online and in front of the screen, which can negatively affect physical health and mental well-being. Computers can also disrupt sleep, as people are often tempted to stay up late engrossed in their online activities. 

Computers can also have a detrimental effect on cognitive development. People’s brains develop in part through conversations with other people, but computers cannot take the place of real-life interactions. Furthermore, the constant influx of information and visuals from the internet can lead to information overload and can decrease the attention span.
