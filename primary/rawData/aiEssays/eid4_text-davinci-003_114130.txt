

At the end of the poem, the speaker is reflecting on the passing of the winter season. They are looking forward to spring and the opportunity to take a test again. The winter hibiscus serves as a metaphor for the speaker's own journey and progress; when it begins to bud and bloom, it symbolizes the speaker's growth and new beginnings. The speaker is looking to the future with hope, expressing that they will take the test again and grow from the experience. Thus, the poem ends with this reflective thought, expressing a new hope for the future despite the hardships of the present.
