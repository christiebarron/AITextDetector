

Narciso Rodriguez’s memoir, From Home: The Blueprints of Our Lives, creates a deeply moving and reflective mood for the reader. Through the personal stories shared in the memoir, Rodriguez takes the reader on a journey of his childhood, growing up in Newark, New Jersey in the 1970s and 80s. He shares his memories of his upbringing between his Cuban mother and Puerto Rican father, and the strong family bond that he felt from a young age. 

The memoir primarily focuses on the themes of family, identity and home. Rodriguez reflects on his experience of growing up between two cultures, and the unique identity it shaped for him. He speaks candidly about the challenges he faced while making his way toward the world of fashion. The memoir follows him as he takes on an internship at a fashion magazine, and his determination to break into the industry. 

The creation of this nostalgic and reflective mood allows for a powerful connection between Rodriguez and the reader. Through his evocative language, he portrays a vivid image of his upbringing and the challenges he faced to achieve his dreams. His writing evokes a sense of nostalgia and longing for home. His passion for fashion, and the drive that it instills in him, gives a feeling of hope and perseverance, and a desire to take ownership of one’s identity and life path.
