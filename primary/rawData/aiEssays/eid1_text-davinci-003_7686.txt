

The last line of Minfong Ho's "Winter Hibiscus" is a metaphor for hope and resilience. The hibiscus is a symbol of fragility and hope at the same time. It is fragile, flowering in the midst of a harsh winter. Yet, it is also a symbol of hope, of perseverance in the face of adversity. This line reflects the main character's determination to overcome her struggles and take the test again in the spring. It conveys a message of hope and encouragement, that one can have the courage to rise again in despite of failure.
