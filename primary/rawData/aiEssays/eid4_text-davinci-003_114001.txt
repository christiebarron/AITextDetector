

The narrator wants to emphasize the lasting effects of her aunt's death. She reflects on the hibiscus flower that remind her of her aunt and how she will take the test again when they come back in the spring. The optimism of the last line is hopeful and shows how the narrator has come to terms with her aunt's death. The returning geese and budding flower symbolize the cycle of life and death and how the narrator will keep moving forward despite her loss.
