

The ending of the poem reflects the speaker's desire for renewal and hope for a better future. The poet uses the image of the hibiscus blooming and the geese returning to represent the possibility of new beginnings and the promise of a better, brighter future. The speaker suggests that, in the same way, if they were to return in the spring and take the test again, then they could experience a new start and a chance to succeed. The poem effectively conveys that no matter how difficult the present situation may be, there is always the possibility of a brighter and healthier future.
