

The ending of Winter Hibiscus is meant to symbolize hope and persistence. The narrator is struggling to pass a math test, and by ending with the hopeful sentiment that the narrator will take the test again when the hibiscus blooms in the spring, Minfong Ho is offering encouragement and optimism in the face of a challenge. The imagery of the melting snow and returning geese represents the passing of winter and the start of a new season, suggesting that with time and effort, the narrator can overcome their struggles and pass the test.
