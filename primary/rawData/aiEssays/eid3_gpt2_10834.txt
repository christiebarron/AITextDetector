Features of the setting affect the cyclist in 'Rough Road Ahead' in many ways. For example, there is a clear difference in the number of places in front and behind when the 'Rough Road Ahead' setting is enabled.

The key difference is the number of places in front and behind that will turn down to make this difficult to achieve, as well as in places that remain open and to keep the bike safe in traffic.

For more information on the setting see: Turning Down the Way Ahead

More Info

It should be noted that the rider can use the following to activate the 'Rough Road Ahead' setting

In some situations, the 'Rough Road Ahead' setting can be used and must be turned off by an action of the rider to ensure the correct turning position is attained

If using the 'Rough Road Ahead' setting without turning out of control it is likely riders will not want to turn out or change corners.

For advice on these options see Turning Down the Way Ahead

This option is also used again by some 'Rough Road Ahead' users to help make cycling safer, especially on the first few streets before the opening of major new roads in 2012.
