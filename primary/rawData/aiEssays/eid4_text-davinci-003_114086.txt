

The ending of Winter Hibiscus provides a message of reinvention and hope. The narrator has faced challenges with the schoolwork, and her grandfather has told her to use the winter hibernation of the plant as an example of how to take a break from things that are difficult. He encourages her to consider the possibilities of starting anew when the time is right. Thus, the ending of the story reflects the idea of perseverance and renewal, leading to future success for the narrator. By suggesting that the protagonist might take her test again when the hibiscus is budding and the signs of spring come back, the grandfather is imparting his wisdom to the protagonist to give her the strength that she needs to keep going.
