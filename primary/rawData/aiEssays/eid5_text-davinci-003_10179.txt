

The memoir From Home: The Blueprints of Our Lives by Narciso Rodriguez is a thought-provoking journey through the author's life and experiences, which paints a vivid picture of what it's like to grow up in a home with Latinx influences and shared family values.

The memoir creates a very emotional and reflective mood for readers from the start. The author paints a vivid picture of his childhood in his family's home in New Jersey. He recalls the vibrant atmosphere that surrounded him, between the noise of the street vendors and passersby, to the smell of his mother's cooking. Through Rodriguez's detailed memiors, readers are able to get a sense of the love and closeness his family had, as he describes how he and his siblings cooked and shared meals together with his parents. The memoir also reflects on the sense of community Rodriguez experienced, and the importance of family in his Latinx heritage.

Rodriguez also shares some of the difficulties he faced growing up, such as not knowing much Spanish, and being an immigrant in a new country. Despite these struggles, he reflects on his experience with a sense of gratitude, sharing that he feels fortunate to have been exposed both to his family's traditions and to broader opportunities.
