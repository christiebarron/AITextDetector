

The line is meant to be a metaphor for the protagonist's journey towards self-enlightenment. The white snows of winter represent her impenetrable ignorance and unwillingness to learn. The melting of the snow represents her shedding of the barriers that prevent her from growth. The geese returning symbolize the transformational change she is undergoing to reach a deeper level of understanding. Finally, the blooming of the hibiscus serves as a representation of her enlightenment and newfound motivation to take the test again, having acquired more knowledge and wisdom than before. In essence, the quote signifies that after she has gone through the necessary steps of self-development, she will be prepared to take the test again and achieve success.
