

The line that ends Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho serves as a metaphor for the protagonist's journey of transformation. In the story, the protagonist is a young woman from a rural Chinese village who moves to America and finds herself in a strange and unfamiliar place. Through struggle and perseverance, she eventually finds her place, as symbolized by her cultivation of a hibiscus in winter. She learns the importance of resilience and growth in adversity, and the line serves to emphasize that feeling of transformation. It serves as a reminder to the protagonist that with change, comes growth, and she is capable of overcoming any difficulty she may face in the future.
