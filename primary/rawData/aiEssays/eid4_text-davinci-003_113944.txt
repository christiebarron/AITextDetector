

The end of Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho emphasizes the message of hope and perseverance that is a theme throughout the story. The narrator is referring to a college entrance examination that she had failed, but despite her disappointment she exhibits a sense of optimism as she looks to the future. The narrator recognizes that while she may have failed the test, she will have another opportunity to take it and succeed. Looking to the future, when the snows melt, the geese return, and the hibiscus buds – all symbols of newness and life – the narrator reminds herself that she can take that test again. The hopeful ending resonates with the reader and emphasizes the importance of continuing to work towards one's goals, despite any setbacks.
