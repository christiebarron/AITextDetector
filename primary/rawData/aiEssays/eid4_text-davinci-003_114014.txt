

The ending of Winter Hibiscus is meant to bring a sense of hope and optimism to the poem. The hibiscus serves–in the poem–as a metaphor for the speaker's struggles and their determination to overcome them. The idea of the hibiscus “budding” at springtime symbolizes a new start and the determination to try again; no matter how difficult the challenges may be. The speaker is expressing their determination to keep trying despite their previous failures, and to keep faith that their efforts will eventually lead to success. The ending of the poem is meant to encourage the reader to have hope and faith that things may not always be as difficult as they seem; if they persevere and keep trying, success may still be within reach.
