

The protagonist has been thinking of taking an English language test and wondering from the start of the story if they would be able to pass it. The rhetorical question at the end of the story is used to illustrate how even in the winter, when the hibiscus is not blooming, there is still hope that the protagonist will do well on this test. It signifies the idea that like the hibiscus, there is the potential for growth and progress, even when it is hard to see hope in the present. The protagonist is expressing faith that better times will come and that they will be prepared for what the future holds.
