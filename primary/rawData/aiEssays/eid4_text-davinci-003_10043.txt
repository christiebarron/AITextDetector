

The closing sentence of Winter Hibiscus, serves as a metaphor for the narrator's determination to take a test again and succeed. It symbolizes the narrator's dedication to improve, despite the challenges she faces. The return of the geese and the budding of the hibiscus shows the persistence of life, even through the winter. Despite the hardships of winter and the struggle of taking the test, the narrator is prepared to take it again once the spring comes and life returns. The imagery of the snows melting and the hibiscus blossoming serves as a reminder that no matter how difficult the situation, there will always be a chance to start again.
