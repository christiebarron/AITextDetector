

The ending of Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho requires the reader to draw on the themes of resilience, determination, and hope that run throughout the story. The conclusion of the story suggests that although the girl is struggling academically and failing her test, she finds strength in nature and its cycles of seasons. By writing that she will take the test again when the snows melt and the hibiscus is in bloom, the author implies that the girl is determined to pass her test and acknowledges that she will be in better shape when the hard times of winter have passed. The ending suggests that with hard work and dedication, the girl can pass her test and her life can take a more positive turn.
