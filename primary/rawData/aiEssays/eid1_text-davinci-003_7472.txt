

The ending line of Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho reflects the protagonist's hope that she can retake the test and succeed in the future. The imagery of the snow melting and geese returning reflects spring, rebirth, and fresh beginnings; the hibiscus budding is symbolic of growth and potential. This ending line reflects the protagonist's determination and desire to be successful, while also conveying the idea that even if one has failed, there is always the hope of a new beginning and another opportunity to try again.
