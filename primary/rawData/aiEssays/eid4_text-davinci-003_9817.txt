

The speaker in "Winter Hibiscus" is a young student who has just taken an important exam during a ruthless winter. The exam results will determine whether or not he can earn a higher education. The exam process has been stressful for the speaker due to his young age. The speaker ends with the hopeful thought that when spring returns and the snow melts, he will take the test again and that the hibiscus flower will be back to an apparent symbol of his hope and strength. By ending with this hopeful thought, the speaker expresses the resilience he has gained through the hardship of taking the test during an unforgiving winter season. His optimism encourages the audience to also remain hopeful and resilient in the face of life's challenges.
