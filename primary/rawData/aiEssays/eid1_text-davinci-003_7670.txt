By returning to the metaphor of the hibiscus used throughout the story, the author shows that, despite the hardships of their exile, Ah-Kim will overcome them. The hibiscus, in this case, symbolizes resilience and hope for the future, offering the promise of a better life. Thus, the ending of the story ties together the themes of resilience and hope, emphasizing the power of these themes to propel one forward even in difficult times.
