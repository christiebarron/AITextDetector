

The ending of "Winter Hibiscus" by Minfong Ho is symbolic of the journey the narrator has been on in the story. Throughout the narrative, the narrator has been struggling with her identity as a Chinese-American, trying to decide whether she should assimilate to the American culture or try to preserve her Chinese cultural identity. the hibiscus, which is resilient in the winter, symbolizes the narrator's strength and resilience in her struggle. The narrator is deciding to take a test that will help her choose her future, and she views the activity as a test. By ending the story with the line "When they come back in the spring, when the snows melt and the geese return and this hibiscus is budding, then I will take that test again," the narrator is expressing her commitment to choose her future identity no matter how difficult it will be. The narrator shows strength and determination in the face of adversity, symbolized by the hibiscus growing back in the spring. The symbolism of the hibiscus blooming in the spring emphasizes the narrator's commitment to taking control of her own life and future.
