

The author of Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho uses the hibiscus as a symbol of hope for a brighter future. At the end of the story, the narrator considers a high school equivalency exam that she must retake in order to demonstrate her academic training. Faced with the difficulty and pressure of the upcoming test, the narrator turns to the hibiscus for strength and guidance. By linking the hibiscus with the passing of winter and the return of spring, the author reinforces the idea that the narrator will be able to confront and surpass the challenges of the exam and ultimately realize her long-held academic dreams.
