

Minfong Ho ends Winter Hibiscus with this line to emphasize the story's themes of resilience and hope. The protagonist is hopeful that if she can survive the winter, she will be able to take the test again in the spring and pass it. The imagery of snow melting, geese returning, and hibiscus budding illustrates how when the cold winter passes, the protagonist will experience a renewal of hope, and be able to take the test and hopefully succeed. This motif of rebirth symbolizes the change and growth that is possible within the human spirit.
