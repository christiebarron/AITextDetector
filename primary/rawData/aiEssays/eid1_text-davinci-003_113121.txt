

Dear Editor,

It's undeniable that computers have had a tremendous impact on our daily lives, both in positive and negative ways. I believe it's time to start a conversation about the human consequences caused by computers and the internet.

With the dawn of the computer age, people are spending more time online than ever before. For many, the internet has become an integral part of their lives. It's a great way to access information, connect with people virtually, and find entertainment on-demand. Unfortunately, this all comes at a cost; excessive use of computers can cause strain on physical and mental health, disconnect us from the real world, and lead to possible addiction. 

Physically speaking, people who spend too much time at a computer can develop nausea, headaches, blurred vision, and disturbing sleep patterns. Unhealthy lifestyles begin to emerge with long, sedentary hours in front of a computer. People forget to take regular breaks and eat healthily.

The rise in online activities can be psychologically damaging too. It can cause feelings of loneliness, low self-esteem, and a false sense of belonging. It can be hard to stick to a healthy routine while online and practice self-care. The excessive use of social media can lead to anxiety and depression when we compare our lives with others.
