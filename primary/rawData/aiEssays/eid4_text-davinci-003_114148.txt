

The passage ends with this line because it is a reminder for the protagonist to take the test again when they return in the spring. This symbolizes perseverance and not giving up in spite of failure. It promotes continued effort and motivation. The imagery of winter hibiscus also serves as a metaphor of hope and possibilities despite adversity. By emphasizing the return of the geese and the blooming hibiscus in the spring, Minfong Ho is expressing the idea that despite the cold winter, warmth and beauty will return soon.
