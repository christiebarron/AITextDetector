

The narrator of this story is a young student who is feeling pressure to do well in school and to ensure her family's continued success. She is dealing with difficult emotions and anxieties, so this ending provides hope that things might be different when the spring returns. The imagery of the snows melting and the geese returning helps to create a sense of renewal and potential. The return of the hibiscus plant reflects the idea that if she remains patient and hopeful during the winter, then come the spring, she can try the test again. This ending serves to provide her with comfort and to reassure her that there is hope for a better outcome.
