The mood created in the memoir 'From Home is' appears to have been that of one single man on a mission to give a message of encouragement to other people.

The first day was a pleasant one: there were no noisy cars or noisy people. The other day some kind of protest was held. It was an act of collective defiance.

'I told my kids over and over: 'I know you're feeling this, you're being affected and you need your voice heard, we need solidarity, we need your involvement in doing something and the way you are dealing with it is very empowering.' I wanted to be able to say that I have a message to them to give.'
