

The builders of the Empire State Building had the ambitious idea of allowing dirigibles to dock on top of the structure. This concept, while novel, was fraught with obstacles and challenges. As outlined in the book “The Mooring Mast” by Marcia Amidon Lüsted, the feat was achieved despite numerous untested challenges.

The most notable challenge for the builders of the Empire State Building was that no such docking systems for dirigibles had ever been created before. This meant that the development of the facility, which included a floating mast constructed atop the building, had to be done from scratch. Unsurprisingly, the builders had to consider numerous safety and engineering challenges before they would allow a giant airship to dock atop one of the most recognizable buildings in the world.

The builders of the Empire State Building went to great lengths to ensure the safety of any dirigible that would dock at the mast. To begin with, the mast was constructed to be durable enough to handle the engines and immense weight of the dirigibles. Additionally, the builders had to ensure that the airships could land on the mast with precision and safety due to the more than 1,000 foot drop to the ground below.
