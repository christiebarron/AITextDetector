

The narrator of Winter Hibiscus, a young girl named Siew-Hwa, is very stressed out due to the pressure she feels from her parents to get good grades in school and make them proud. She is also struggling with loneliness, as she often feels overwhelmed by her family's high expectations and cannot cope on her own. At the end of the story, Siew-Hwa finds solace in the winter hibiscus given to her by her grandmother. As the hibiscus stands strong through the cold of winter, Siew-Hwa is comforted by its resilience and optimism for the future. The message is that, with strength and determination, Siew-Hwa will also be able to get through the difficult times and will be able to take her test again in the springtime, when the snow has melted and the birds have returned to offer a new beginning.
