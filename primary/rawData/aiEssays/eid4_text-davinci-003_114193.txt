

The ending of the poem provokes thought and reflection in the reader. The last line asks the reader to consider the importance of perseverance. By tying perseverance to natural occurrences, such as the snows melting and the geese returning, the author conveys a message of hope during difficult times. It encourages the reader to stay determined in the face of adversity, as nature will eventually bring back better times. Thus, this last line ultimately serves as an encouragement to strive for one's goals, despite any initial obstacles that may come their way.
