

At the end of Winter Hibiscus, the protagonist is reflecting on the end of winter and the upcoming spring. She feels anxious about the upcoming test she will have to take in the spring, but she finds strength in the possibility of a new season. The hibiscus, her only solace in the winter months, will bloom again in the spring, offering her a symbol of hope. By ending the poem in this way, the protagonist is able to look ahead to the possibility of a fresh start and a renewed sense of determination.
