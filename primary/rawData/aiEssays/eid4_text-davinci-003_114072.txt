

The ending to Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho serves to emphasize the theme of hope and perseverance in the face of uncertainty. By ending with the protagonist's determination to retake the test in the spring, the author conveys a message of resilience and faith in a better future. The elements of nature mentioned (the snows melting, the geese returning, and the hibiscus budding) serve as symbols of renewal and hope; they remind the protagonist that difficult times don't last forever, and that with persistence and belief in oneself, even seemingly insurmountable obstacles can be overcome.
