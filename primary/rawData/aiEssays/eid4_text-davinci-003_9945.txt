

The ending of the short story Winter Hibiscus provides a sense of hope and optimism for the narrator's future. The narrator, Jeannie, is struggling with the difficult transition from being a child to becoming an adult, and is worried about an upcoming test in her "Citizenship and Civics" class. By linking her return to test-taking with the renewal of life and nature that occurs in the springtime, Minfong Ho suggests that Jeannie too will persevere and overcome the challenges she faces. The imagery of melting snow, geese returning, and a budding hibiscus combine to create a hopeful image for Jeannie's future, conveying the message that her growth and transformation will bring her success.
