

As a society, we have long held the belief that everyone should have the right to access information freely. Libraries have traditionally been a place where people can go to learn, explore, and find new ideas. Unfortunately, censorship continues to exist in libraries, limiting access to information for members of the public. As a concerned citizen, I strongly believe that censorship of materials in libraries should not be allowed and that some materials, such as books, music, movies, and magazines, should not be removed from the shelves if they are found to be offensive.

To begin, censorship violates the right to free expression guaranteed under the First Amendment. By censoring books, movies, music, and magazines from library shelves, we are essentially denying individuals their right to freedom of information. When a person visits the library, they are not required to check out or support any particular type of material, so denying them access to certain materials is a violation of their civil liberties. It is also important to remember that even though the materials in a library are available to anyone, it is ultimately up to the individual to make the decision to borrow it or not.
