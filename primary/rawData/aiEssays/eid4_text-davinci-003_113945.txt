

The end of the poem Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho emphasizes the idea of resilience and hope. The speaker expresses that, despite a difficult experience, they are determined to try again. The imagery of melting snow, returning geese, and a budding hibiscus reflects the idea of new beginnings, bringing a sense of optimism to the poem. The speaker is leaving the reader with a sense of hope and courage, encouraging them to strive even in the face of adversity.
