

To the Editor,

In today’s world, computers have become commonplace in homes, workplaces, and schools. While they have changed our lives in many ways, the rapid technological progress they have enabled us is not without its downsides.

The use of computers has caused people to become less social and more isolated from the outside world. People can easily spend hours of their day alone in front of a computer screen, only interacting with the virtual world instead of the real people and places around them. In addition, research has shown that the long-term use of computers and other digital devices can have adverse effects on people’s physical and mental health. These include eyestrain, headaches, neck and back pain, and even psychological effects such as depression and anxiety.

At the same time, computers are an integral part of our lives, and the internet provides us with a great many useful opportunities for communication, collaboration, and entertainment. The key is to use computers in moderation and be mindful of the potential side-effects.

It is critical for people, especially young people, to be aware of these potential dangers and take steps to protect themselves. Parents, teachers, and other adults must set an example
