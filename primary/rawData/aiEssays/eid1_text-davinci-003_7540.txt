

The protagonist of the story is a young girl named Saya who has failed a school test and is embarrassed by it. The ending of the story serves to give Saya hope and remind her that everything is not lost. The symbolism of the hibiscus growing back in the spring symbolizes Saya's ability to come back from her failure and try again. She has been told by her grandmother that every flower has its own season, and the hibiscus reminds her to keep her chin up and try again when the time comes. Her grandmother has given her a gift of strength and hope and Saya knows that when the time comes she will take her test again.
