

The ending of Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho is intended to emphasize the resilience, perseverance, and hope of the protagonist. The protagonist has faced difficult decisions, discrimination, and fear throughout the book, but they remain hopeful that things will get better in the future- symbolized by the hibiscus blossoming in the spring. In their act of determination to try again, the protagonist demonstrates their strength and resolve. In a broader sense, the conclusion serves as a reminder that difficult times can be faced with resilience and that hope is eternal.
