

Minfong Ho's Winter Hibiscus ends with this hopeful statement as a way to show the reader that no matter what happens in life, there is always an opportunity for rebirth and a chance to start over. The image of the hibiscus is used as a metaphor for hope; it blooms every year despite the cold and snow, representing that no matter the difficult challenges that life brings, one can come out of it stronger and more resilient. By having the protagonist, a high schooler, express the thought that she will take the test again in the spring suggests that no matter what her score may be the first time, there are other opportunities to do better and reach her goals.
