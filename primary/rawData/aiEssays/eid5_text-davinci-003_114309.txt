

The memoir From Home: The Blueprints of Our Lives by Narciso Rodriguez captures the memories and impressions of the designer’s childhood growing up in Newark, New Jersey. Rodriguez paints a vivid picture of his upbringing, providing an intimate look at his formative years. Through his writing, Rodriguez creates a unique mood, one that evokes nostalgia, wonderment, and appreciation for the importance of family.

The mood of the memoir is established right from the start, as the reader is immersed in the sights, sounds, scents, and textures of the Newark landscape that formed the foundation of Rodriguez’s formative years. As he leads the reader through his childhood memories of family life—of “ceaseless Sunday dinners, the salt-lined heat of summer barbecues, the lush smells of freshly cooked dishes”—a sense of nostalgia is created. There is a wistful feel as Rodriguez reflects on the meaningful moments he experienced with his family and all the ways in which those moments shaped his life.

Throughout the memoir, there is a sense of awe and appreciation at how Rodriguez’s parents interacted with the world whether it was through their jobs, their friendships, or their intellectual and cultural pursuits. The reader is thus struck by the power of the family’s influence, and the valuable lessons and perspectives they imparted.
