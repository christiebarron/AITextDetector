The speaker of the poem is reflecting on her village's drought, which has caused an incessant thirst and seemingly unrelenting hardships. In the final line of the poem, the speaker expresses hope that perhaps the spring will bring respite from their struggle, symbolized by the budding hibiscus flower. She reflects that when she sees the flowers growing again, she will be reminded of her strength and be ready to try again. This final line conveys the idea that if nature can be resilient during the winter's adversity, so too can they.
