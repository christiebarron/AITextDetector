

The Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho ends with this reflection, so that the reader can contemplate the idea of courage and perseverance in spite of adversity. The narrator of the story is an immigrant struggling to learn English in order to pass a test that will allow her to become a citizen. The ending reflects her determination to keep trying even if she doesn't pass the test the first time. The imagery of the snows melting and the geese returning is a metaphor for hope and resilience in the face of difficulty. The hibiscus flower symbolizes the narrator's determination to blossom even in the face of a wintery challenge. By ending with this hopeful reflection, the narrator is hoping to inspire the reader with her story and courage.
