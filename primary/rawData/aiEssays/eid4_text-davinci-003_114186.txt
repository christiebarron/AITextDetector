

The ending of Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho allows the protagonist to take ownership of her own learning. She is determined to face a test again and is not defeated by her initial failure. The imagery of the melting snow and returning geese give the protagonist hope that she might eventually be able to pass the test and come out triumphant. The ending also serves to build tension and suspense, as the reader wants to know if she will succeed after the season changes. By using this imagery, Minfong Ho gives us an understanding of the character's fortitude and resilience, demonstrating that failure doesn't have to mark the end of a journey.
