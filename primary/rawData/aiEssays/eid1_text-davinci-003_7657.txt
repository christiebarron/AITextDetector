

The ending of Minfong Ho’s “Winter Hibiscus” reflects the hopefulness of its narrative. By ending with the narrator’s pledge to take the same test again when the snows melt and the geese return, Ho conveys that there is always hope and the potential for progress and new beginnings. This imagery of the snows melting and the geese returning serves as a metaphor for a new cycle starting, representing the narrator’s bravery and determination to confront her fears and move forward. In addition, the image of the hibiscus budding conveys the idea of growth and renewal, creating a sense of hope and optimism and reinforcing the idea that the narrator can and will persevere despite her fears and worries.
