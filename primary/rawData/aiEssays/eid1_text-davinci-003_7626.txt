

The ending of Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho provides a hopeful conclusion to the poem. Despite the fact that the narrator feels the weight of failure for not passing a test, the narrator looks forward to the springtime when the snow melts, the geese return, and the hibiscus buds. This imagery symbolizes a time of growth and possibility, where the narrator can try again and hopefully pass the test. The narrator is expressing optimism, even in the face of the past difficulty and disappointment. The ending conveys a message of resilience, and suggests that the narrator has hope that things will improve in the future.
