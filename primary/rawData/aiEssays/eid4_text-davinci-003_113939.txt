

The ending of Winter Hibiscus is meant to be a source of hope, as it highlights the cyclical nature of life. The narrator's journey is one of growth and resilience. By having the narrator look forward to the future, Ho conveys the idea that, despite all the struggles the narrator and her family face, they will be able to recover and continue growing. The ending illustrates the idea that despite all the struggles and hardships we face, taking a step back and looking to the future can bring us peace and hope. By having the narrator plan for the future and renew their hope for better times, they can continue to keep pushing through the present while looking forward to the future.
