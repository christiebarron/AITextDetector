

The ending of Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho mirrors the characters' experience of transformation and growth. At the beginning of the story, the narrator fails a test because of her fear and insecurity. By the end, through her sister and the hibiscus, she has gained a newfound confidence. The line "When they come back in the spring, when the snows melt and the geese return and this hibiscus is budding, then I will take that test again" gives the reader a sense of hope and possibility that the narrator has experienced a personal transformation that will help her with that test. The line brings the story full circle, showing that although the narrator has overcome her fear and insecurity, the journey is ongoing.
