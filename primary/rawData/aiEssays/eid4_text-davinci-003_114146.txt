

The story ends with the protagonist reflecting on their experience of taking a standardized test, and the feeling of being alone in a foreign environment in the midst of overwhelming expectations. The protagonist realizes that, although they felt alone while taking the test, there were still people around them, and that with effort, they can find a place in the world as they embraced a new culture. The ending with the promise of the hibiscus budding and the geese returning gives the reader hope: even after a difficult experience, the protagonist is looking forward to doing better the next time. The hibiscus serves as a symbol of renewal and resilience. The natural beauty of the season and the promise of a second attempt reflect a sense of hope for the future.
