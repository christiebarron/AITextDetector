

Minfong Ho ends Winter Hibiscus with the protagonist's reflections on transformation and resilience. By asking the reader to imagine the protagonist retaking a test in the spring when the river thaws, wildlife returns, and a cherished hibiscus plant buds, Ho implies that the protagonist will be ready to take the test and succeed in the spring. This suggests that the protagonist has grown and changed in meaningful ways over the course of their journey, and emphasizes the idea that renewal and transformation are possible when navigating difficult times.
