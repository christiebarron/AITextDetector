

Minfong Ho, the author of Winter Hibiscus, ends the story with this line to represent the protagonist's hope. She has taken an important exam, believing that the results will help her to have a better future. The protagonist's hope is symbolized by the hibiscus. The line at the end of the story shows that the protagonist is looking ahead to the end of winter and the spring when the hibiscus blooms and takes on new life. It is in this season of renewal that the protagonist is confident she can take the test again, knowing that the results will eventually lead to a better future.
