

The author of Winter Hibiscus, Minfong Ho, uses this final sentence serves as both a conclusion and an invitation to hope. The imagery of the coming of spring symbolizes a new beginning, and the return of the geese and the budding of the hibiscus implies the promise of renewal. This is echoed in the story's young protagonist, who is determined to retake a challenging test in the springtime despite the challenges she faced during the winter. In this way, the line serves as an optimistic reminder that difficult things can be overcome with perseverance.
