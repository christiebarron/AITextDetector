As technology advances, computers have become more and more a part of everyday life. While computers offer many advantages, they too have their downfalls in how they have changed our society. 

First and foremost, computers have changed the way people interact with one another. Texting and video-calling have largely replaced face-to-face contact in most forms of communication. This has led to an increase in misunderstandings due to the lack of body language, facial expressions, and other nonverbal cues. In addition, impersonal messaging has created a decrease in motivation for people to connect with each other more, which likely contributes to feelings of loneliness and social disconnection.

Furthermore, computers can lead to an increase in productivity, but people can become so reliant on them that they take away the ability to perform basic tasks and use problem-solving skills. Computers have become a crutch for people, and over-reliance on them can lead to reduced creativity and a lack of critical thinking skills.
