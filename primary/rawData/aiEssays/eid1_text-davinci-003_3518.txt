

Censorship is one of the most controversial and debated topics in today’s society. While some may view censorship as necessary for protecting people from offensive content, others have argued that it infringes on a person’s freedom of expression. The controversy extends beyond television, movies, and magazines and extends to libraries, where materials such as books, music, movies, and magazines should be accessible to the public. 

Library censorship has been a heated topic of debate for years. On one hand, there are those who think that a library should take it upon itself to filter out any questionable material, and censor it accordingly. This group may think that certain materials, such as books, music, movies, and magazines, should be removed from the shelves if they are found to be offensive. On the other hand, there are those who believe that it is the role of a library to retain all materials, regardless of any potential offensiveness it may contain. According to these people, it should be up to the patron to decide whether or not they are comfortable accessing and viewing the material.
