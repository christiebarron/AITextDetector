

The ending of the poem reflects the narrator's hope for a brighter future, as represented by the blooming winter hibiscus. The blooming hibiscus symbolizes the growth and renewal the narrator is looking forward to both mentally and emotionally. By ending the poem this way, the narrator is expressing their faith that eventually things will become better and success will be achieved, just like how the winter hibiscus will bloom in the spring. The narrator's belief that success is achievable is also reflected in the line "When they come back in the spring, when the snows melt and the geese return and this hibiscus is budding, then I will take that test again?" This line represents the narrator's determination to persist, and signals that success is possible despite the difficult times that may lie ahead.
