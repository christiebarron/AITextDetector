

Narciso Rodriguez's memoir 'From Home: The Blueprints of Our Lives' is a compilation of poignant images, poignant thoughts, and powerful emotions that draw the reader in from the very first chapter. Throughout the memoir, Rodriguez paints a vivid picture of his childhood and family life in the city of Newark, New Jersey. Through his soft, evocative voice, he captures the sights, smells, and sounds of his life, giving an intimate portrait of the experiences that shaped his formative years. 

Each of the chapters throughout the memoir serve to draw in the reader deep into the story, almost as if they were there as well. By using a series of vivid metaphors, Rodriguez paints a vivid portrait of his life and the feeling it invoked. The vibrancy of Newark's culture is captured beautifully in Rodriguez's words, as he speaks of the sights and sounds of his vibrant city. Moreover, his memories of family, both close and extended, give readers a very personal look into the bond and strength of his family relationships.

The overall mood of the memoir is one of nostalgia, where the reader can share in the joys and sorrows of Rodriguez's life. There is a melancholic overtone that is captured throughout, as the author reflects on his family's many struggles and hardships they overcame.
