

The author is suggesting that the Winter Hibiscus serves as a symbol of hope and perseverance for the protagonist. By ending the story with this, the author implies that the protagonist is confident in his ability to improve himself and that there will be a brighter future ahead of him. The metaphor of the Winter Hibiscus coming back in the spring and blooming is a reminder that with patience and effort, anything is possible. The protagonist has already learned important lessons about himself and is prepared to take on any upcoming challenges with renewed hope and optimism.
