

Dear Editor,

The technology revolution of the past few decades has made computers an important part of our lives, helping to serve our social, professional, and educational needs. Though I’m a big advocate of technology, I think it’s important to recognize the potential drawbacks of computers and the impact they can have on our society.

At the most basic level, computers can be distracting in the workplace or classroom, leading to a decrease in productivity. Constant access to the digital world can also lead to feelings of loneliness and detachment, replacing real-time face-to-face interactions with superficial online exchanges. Additionally, computers have become entrenched in our lives in ways we might not have anticipated a few decades ago. From facial recognition software to GPS tracking, computers are passing judgment on us, monitoring our movements and thoughts in ways that can feel intrusive.

While computers are an important tool for enhancing our ability to communicate, access information, and complete tasks, it’s important to be aware of the potential downside. Technology can be an incredibly positive force in our lives, but it’s important to be mindful of the way it’s being used and the impact it can have.
