

The ending of Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho is symbolic of the protagonist's resilience and determination despite the difficulty of the situation they are facing. The protagonist has been struggling in school and is feeling overwhelmed, but despite this they are determined to try again despite the feeling that the odds are against them. The imagery of the returning geese, melting snow, and budding hibiscus is a metaphor for the renewal of hope as winter transitions to spring. By the end of the story, the protagonist is filled with new strength and is determined to take the test again. The imagery of the snow melting and the geese returning symbolizes the passing of adversity and the hopefulness of transition and renewal.
