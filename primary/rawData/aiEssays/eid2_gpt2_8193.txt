The NDP has been a thorn in Trudeau's side since he took office last summer, when they campaigned on "free expression" and "unrestricted access" to public documents.

The party's chief critic Bob Rae said "this is just like getting attacked on Twitter: the government is being very bad at protecting speech that should have been protected in all societies, if not under Canada's Constitution."

"They are the only political parties that can do this and that means that we don't have an easy way to defend our intellectual property rights."
