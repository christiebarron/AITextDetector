

The ending of Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho serves to emphasize the narrator's determination to confront the challenges she faces with her life in the United States as an Asian immigrant. The imagery of the hibiscus coming back in the spring, the snows melting and the geese returning, all seem to serve as symbols for resilience and hope. The narrator is determined to take the test again, viewing it as a symbol of her progress and growth. It is a statement of hope and a reminder that she can still succeed in spite of her struggles.
