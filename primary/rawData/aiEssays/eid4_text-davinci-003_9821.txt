

The author of the poem, Minfong Ho, wants to leave readers with the idea that no matter how hard or impossible something may seem, they should never give up and that they should always remain hopeful. The last line of the poem implies that the speaker will keep trying and will not give up. By mentioning the snows melting and the geese returning, the speaker is emphasizing the idea of renewal and hope. The speaker's willingness to keep taking the test even if they already failed shows the theme of resilience and perseverance.
