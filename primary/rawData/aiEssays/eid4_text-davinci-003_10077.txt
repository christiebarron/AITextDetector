

The narrator is referencing the cycle of life in the ending of Winter Hibiscus. The narrator sees the hibiscus outside her window and recalls her struggles with school and the upcoming test she must take. She reflects on how winter is a time of death but also of new beginnings. By mentioning the return of the geese and the blooming of the hibiscus, the narrator is emphasizing the idea of hope, renewal, and another chance. The ending of the story implies that the narrator is confident that in a few months, she will have the opportunity to take the test again and do better.
