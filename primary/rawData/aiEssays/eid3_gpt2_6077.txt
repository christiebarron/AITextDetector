Features of the setting affect the cyclist in 'Rough Road Ahead' in many ways.

The cyclist has to make a significant adjustment when approaching some areas of the road or trying to overtake a moving car or bus

The cyclist can make a further adjustment when making a different turn

If a cyclist has made such a leap of faith then it is possible to learn more aboutBrondby and the road experience on this site. This book was developed as part of a series of lectures on the use of data from the National Road Safety Centre at the University of Cambridge, with the emphasis on information on "street life and cycling", rather than "street cycling and road safety".
