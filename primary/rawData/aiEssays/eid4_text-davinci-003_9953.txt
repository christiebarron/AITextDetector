

The speaker in the poem is reflecting on the hardships of taking a challenging test. The hibiscus flower, which represents hope and resilience, is blooming with the coming of spring. The speaker finds solace and peace in nature, and sees the life of the flower as a reminder of strength and determination. In the poem, the speaker draws a parallel between the budding hibiscus and their own future potential. The speaker finds strength and confidence in the flower's endurance, and finds motivation to take the test again in the spring. The winter hibiscus is a reminder that our difficulties will pass, and that we can overcome any challenge. The beautiful imagery of strength and renewal found in the hibiscus, therefore, is fitting to close the poem on a note of hope and possibility.
