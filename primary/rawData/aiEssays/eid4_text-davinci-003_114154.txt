

The ending of Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho is meant to serve as a metaphor for resilience and hopefulness. The hibiscus serves as a symbol of perseverance, as despite the cold weather, it continues to blossom at the end of the story. The narrator also finds encouragement from the hibiscus and uses it to find the strength to take the test again. This is symbolic of her determination to succeed despite challenges. By ending with the narrator's hopeful attitude, the author sends a strong message of resilience and optimism.
