

At the end of Winter Hibiscus, the protagonist talks about how she feels she has failed in her attempts to show her parents the respect that they do not readily accept from her. Through the metaphor of a hibiscus flower, the protagonist is able to express her feelings of hope and optimism. By equating her passing of the upcoming test to the return of spring, the protagonist is conveying her commitment to working hard to do better in the future. In doing so, she will ultimately be able to prove to her parents that she can be successful, and that they should value her efforts and dedication despite their disagreements.
