The cyclist is situated on a long desert road, where the vast expanse of empty space is interrupted by the occasional “unyielding” boulder or “lonely” cactus. Although this setting might appear quite peaceful, it feels oppressive and isolating due to its seemingly infinite presence, and so could be emotionally draining for the cyclist. 

The fact that the cyclist is alone, and that there is no other human presence in sight, further contributes to a sense of isolation and helplessness. The cyclist has no one to rely on but himself, and must push through any difficulty he encounters.
