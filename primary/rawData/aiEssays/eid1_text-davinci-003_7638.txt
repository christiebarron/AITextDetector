

The ending of Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho suggests that the narrator has a hope and a determination to face their future despite the uncertainty and difficulty of the present. The metaphor of the hibiscus gives a sense of hope that even in winter, life can come again. The narrator is determined to take the test again, despite the winter, so that they can grow and look forward to a better future. The ending suggests that even in difficult times, a person can have courage, hope, and resilience.
