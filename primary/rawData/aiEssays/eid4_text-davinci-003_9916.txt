

The protagonist of Winter Hibiscus is a young immigrant girl who has just moved with her family from Thailand to the United States. She is struggling to adjust to her new home and feel accepted at her new school. Through studying with her father, she learns about the winter hibiscus, a delicate flower that thrives in cold climates. The winter hibiscus serves as a metaphor for the protagonist's resilience in persevering through difficult times and adapting to her new environment.

The line at the end of the poem speaks to the hope that the protagonist has for regrowing and adapting, and her determination to take the challenge of her new environment and find success. The winter hibiscus is a sign of hope, something that will return in the spring and signify that the future can be full of brighter possibilities. The line serves as a reminder that anything is possible, even in the midst of adversity.
