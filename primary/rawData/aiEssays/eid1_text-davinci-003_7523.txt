

The protagonist in Winter Hibiscus is a student who feels overwhelmed by her studies and the pressure of taking a tough upcoming test. The ending line is symbolic of her journey to find the strength and resilience to take the test. It is a reminder of the beauty of the natural world and how it has inspired her to find her own strength and forge ahead. The imagery of snow melting, geese returning, and the hibiscus budding represents a time of transformation and growth, which the protagonist recognizes and draws strength from. By connecting this time of growth to her studies, she is made aware of her own strength and potential to push forward and take the test without fear.
