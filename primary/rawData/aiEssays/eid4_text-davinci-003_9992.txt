

The ending of the poem serves as a metaphor for the speaker's hope for revival and a fresh start. The hibiscus flowers are a metaphor for the speaker's confidence and will to succeed. Despite the winter season of adversity and challenge, the speaker can take solace in the fact that come spring time, the hibiscus will return and he will be in a better position to take the test again. The ending emphasizes the idea of never giving up and finding the hope to keep pushing through difficult times.
