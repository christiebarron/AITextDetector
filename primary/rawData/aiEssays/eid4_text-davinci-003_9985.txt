

The ending of the poem is meant to symbolically represent the journey of life and the idea that things can and do come full circle. The narrator of the poem is taking a test and is thinking of the inevitable circle of life, represented by the hibiscus, and the spring season brings new life and hope. The reference to the geese conveys the idea of the unstoppable journey of the seasons, which no matter what, will always come again. The narrator is suggesting that no matter the outcome of the test, things in life will come around again.
