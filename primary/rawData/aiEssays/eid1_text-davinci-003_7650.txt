

The narrator hopes that the hibiscus will be a reminder of how far he has come and how much he has achieved. He is facing a difficult test and he wants to use this flower as a symbol of hope and resilience - that no matter what, he can face any challenge and prevail. The narrator has worked hard to succeed, and he wants to use the hibiscus as a reminder that no matter how hard things may seem, he will be able to come out of it better, and stronger, than when he started. The hibiscus symbolizes the potential for growth and overcoming obstacles. The ending of this poem serves to remind the reader of the importance of perseverance and hope.
