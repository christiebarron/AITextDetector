

The author is trying to convey a message of optimism and hope. The protagonist has faced many hardships and has failed a test, but they have been reminded by the memory of the winter hibiscus that there is always a chance to try again. The line implies that the protagonist will be able to take the test again and likely pass it when the winter hibiscus blossoms in the spring. The metaphor of the hibiscus and its rebirth every spring conveys the idea that life will go on, bringing new opportunities and possibilities for the protagonist to try and succeed.
