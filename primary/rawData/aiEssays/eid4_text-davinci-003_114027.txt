

Minfong Ho ends Winter Hibiscus with this line because it brings full-circle the themes of hope, resilience, and perseverance that run through the entire narrative. The protagonist has faced multiple obstacles throughout the spring and winter, but has managed to get through them all with the help of her family and her own courage and determination. The hibiscus in the story is used as a metaphor for life's challenges and her willingness to take on difficult tasks, and the final line emphasizes the idea that life is never over, no matter how bad things may seem. It communicates that any setback is temporary, and that she will keep trying until she succeeds.
