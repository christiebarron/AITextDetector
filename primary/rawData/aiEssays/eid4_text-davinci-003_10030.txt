

The narrator of Winter Hibiscus is a young Chinese girl living in America who is struggling to learn English while juggling other cultural expectations. The hibiscus in the story symbolizes the narrator's determination to overcome cultural and language barriers. The ending indicates that although she may not have passed the language test at the time, she remains hopeful for the future and resolved to try again. The imagery of the returning geese and the blooming hibiscus reinforce the idea of renewal and new beginnings.
