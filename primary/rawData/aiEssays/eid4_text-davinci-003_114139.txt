

The ending of the poem "Winter Hibiscus" by Minfong Ho symbolizes the narrator's hope that the next season will bring a chance for redemption. The hibiscus, which is a flower typically associated with the spring, serves as a metaphor for their chance to start over, to "take that test again." The narrator is inspired by the prospect of renewal and the cycle of nature that shows each season brings a chance for something new. By ending on this hopeful note, the narrator is able to face the future with strength and optimism.
