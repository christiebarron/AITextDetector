

The symbolism of the winter hibiscus is that the cold and difficult seasons of life can be overcome. By ending the poem in this manner, Ho conveys hope and the idea that hardships don't last forever. The winter hibiscus and the speaker's test provide metaphors for perseverance and determination. Despite the cold and difficulty, both will endure and bring hope for the future. The speaker also conveys a message that with perseverance and determination, even difficult tasks can be overcome in the future.
