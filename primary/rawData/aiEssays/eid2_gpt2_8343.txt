The new move is not expected to sway major political parties.

Labour's shadow Brexit campaigner Mark Reckless said: "This is no different from a totalitarian state where the control of the media and the control of the power structure are considered to be legitimate. That was clear when Labour's decision to ban 'white privilege' was announced last month - this is more disturbing now."

Meanwhile, a spokesman for National, the Labour party that represents about half of the 1.4 million university students in Scotland, said: "There are some really strong reasons why we shouldn't support this kind of censorship."
