

Dear Editor,

As we continue to move forward in an ever-evolving digital world, it is increasingly important to evaluate the effects devices like computers have on our lives and our accomplishments.

In my opinion, computers can have both positive and negative impacts on our existence. On the one hand, they make our lives more efficient and productive—we can complete tasks in a fraction of the time it used to take, share news and knowledge more quickly, and keep in touch with friends, family and colleagues from anywhere in the world.

On the other hand, however, overuse of technology can become a hassle. As we rely on computers more and more, we become more dependent on them—which can lead to a lack of creativity and reduce the amount of time we spend doing physical activities. It also can lead to a decrease in personal interaction and sometimes loneliness.

Thus, it is important for us to find a balance between using computers productively and efficiently and still being able to take part in activities with others and be engaged with the physical world around us. This will help strengthen our relationships, increase our ability to be creative and innovate, and make us healthier both mentally and physically.
