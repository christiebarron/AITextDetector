

Minfong Ho ends Winter Hibiscus with this line because it serves as a metaphor for the protagonist's determination to continue trying, even in the face of failure. The protagonist has failed the test, but they refuse to give up; instead, they decide to try again when the spring comes and conditions are more favorable. This hopeful outlook communicates the importance of resilience and perseverance, even when the odds seem insurmountable. It also emphasizes the idea that there is always another chance; the hopefulness of the line is meant to motivate the reader to continue striving in whatever they do, no matter how difficult the journey may be.
