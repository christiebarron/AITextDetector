

The ending of Winter Hibiscus brings closure to the narrative arc while managing to capture the complex realities of one's experience of displacement. Through this simple ending, Ho attempts to capture the feelings of renewal and resilience of immigrants in the face of adversity. By ending with the protagonist taking a test again in the spring, she draws attention to the idea of every winter having a way of bringing about new beginnings and issuing challenges that must be overcome in order to grow. In this way, the protagonist of Winter Hibiscus has been transformed, and the reader is left with the knowledge that she will persist in spite of her difficulties.
