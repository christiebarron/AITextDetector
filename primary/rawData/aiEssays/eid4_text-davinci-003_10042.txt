

The ending of Minfong Ho's Winter Hibiscus is a hopeful one. It's the narrator's affirmation that she will stay positive and determined despite the difficulties she faces. She knows she has the strength to overcome the obstacles she has faced and she will take the test again when the weather changes. The thought of spring—a time of new beginnings—is a metaphor for the narrator's journey to success and triumph in the face of all the odds. It serves as an empowering message of hope and perseverance for readers.
