

The narrator of the poem is attempting to convey the idea that hardships can be temporary. Even when it seems as if things won't ever change for the better, spring eventually comes. The narrator is speaking about a hibiscus, which represents their struggles. Even though they're feeling defeated, when the winter passes and the flowers are in bloom, they'll find the strength to try again and take the test. By ending the poem with this message of hope, the narrator is encouraging themself and the reader to stay hopeful and to never give up.
