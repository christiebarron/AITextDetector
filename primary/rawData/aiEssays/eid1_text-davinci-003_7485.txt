

The ending of Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho is meant to symbolize the resilience of the protagonist's spirit even in the face of overwhelming academic and personal challenges. The hibiscus blooms in winter despite the harsh conditions, serving as a metaphor for the protagonist's perseverance, determination, and optimism. The final line of the poem serves as a reminder that, no matter how difficult the task may seem, the protagonist will persist and overcome her obstacles when she is ready.
