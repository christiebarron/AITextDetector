

The ending of the poem implies that the speaker is hopeful that taking the test will bring positive outcomes in the future. The buds of the hibiscus signify new beginnings, in this case the hope for a better future with a positive outcome. By referencing the return of spring, the melting of the snow, and the return of the geese, it suggests that the speaker is looking forward to the life and opportunities that come with warmer weather, free of the constraints of winter. The use of the flower and season as metaphor imply that the speaker believes her efforts will be rewarded in the future.
