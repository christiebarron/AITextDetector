

The ending serves as a metaphor for the protagonist's anxiety over taking an upcoming test, and the hope that she will be able to pass it. The season of spring is a time of renewal and growth, and the author uses it to portray the idea that things can get better and that the protagonist can still achieve her goals. By linking the hibiscus blooming to her own potential success, the author creates a hopeful message that with hope and determination, anything can be achieved.
