

The ending of Winter Hibiscus serves as both a hopeful and reassuring finish. By ending on an optimistic note, the protagonist's newfound courage and perseverance are acknowledged, as he is determined to make a comeback after his initial failure. The phrase 'when they come back in the spring, when the snows melt and the geese return and this hibiscus is budding' serves as a metaphor for a new beginning that is on the horizon. It also reinforces the idea of renewal and growth that is previously established in the story, symbolizing the protagonist's willingness to give something new a try. Lastly, the promise to take the test again symbolizes the protagonist's newfound courage to take on a challenge and try again despite what he went through.
