The group includes the National School Book Foundation: a research charity backed by the White House, National Science Foundation, and the National Endowment for the Humanities, which has been involved in curating the nation's top textbooks.

Last month, the American Civil Liberties Union (ACLU) filed a lawsuit with the National Center for Law and Justice to block books on the rights of children from going to school.

The groups say books that include such content could violate free speech and, as such, violate public interest interests.

"In my view, in a number of American schools the right of individual choice does not extend to the right to choose a school over the right to a school," EFF said in a statement.

"Instead, the court said that a school shouldn't be protected from freedom of expression just because it's a place for an older, more powerful, and diverse group of students," it added.
