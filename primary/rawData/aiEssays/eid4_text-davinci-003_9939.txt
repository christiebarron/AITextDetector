

The ending of Winter Hibiscus serves to recall the theme of hope and renewal that is prevalent throughout the poem. The speaker is expressing optimism that they will be able to try again in the future and that the hibiscus flower that symbolizes resilience and strength will bloom and signify new beginnings and possibilities. This ending reflects the idea that this poem is not only about winter but also about the subsequent promise of spring, conveying the comforting knowledge that there is always something to look forward to.
