

Narciso Rodriguez's memoir, From Home: The Blueprints of Our Lives, creates an atmosphere of longing and nostalgia. Throughout the memoir, Rodriguez paints a vivid picture of his childhood memories, from growing up in Puerto Rico to the family life he shared with his mother, father, and brother in the United States.

Rodriguez's descriptions often make the people and places he writes about come alive. His words evoke a sense of longing for the places he left behind, and the reader can feel his nostalgia for his homeland. He writes of the smells and sounds that remind him of his Puerto Rican home, such as the smell of mangoes, the sound of the surf, and the sight of palm trees. His writing style creates a sense of warmth and longing for the people, places, and memories of his youth.

The overall tone of the memoir is one of hopefulness, as Rodriguez is able to look back on his past fondly. His story is one of resilience and growth, which is evidenced in the perseverance he showed while trying to create a better future for himself and his family. He reflects on his life lessons and how they have shaped him as he continues his journey.
