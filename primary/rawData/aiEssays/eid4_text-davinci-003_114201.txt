

The ending of Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho is meant to serve as a metaphor for resilience and hope. The protagonist in the story, Siew Lan, is studying for an important and difficult test. Though she is struggling to learn the necessary material, she persists, stubbornly clinging to the hope that when the winter ends and spring returns, her hard work will pay off and she will be able to take the test. The ending is meant to convey to readers that even when things look bleak, with patience and dedication, it is possible have a success.
