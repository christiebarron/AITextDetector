

Narciso Rodriguez's memoir, From Home: The Blueprints of Our Lives, paints a vivid picture of his childhood and upbringing that affects the overall mood of the book. Rodriguez immerses his reader in his familial history, giving insight into the beliefs, struggles, and traditions of his Dominican American community.

The memoir is filled with a sense of nostalgia and homesickness, which is heightened by the family stories that Rodriguez shares. He tells of his grandmother's struggles in Cuba and his parents’ experience immigrating to the United States. The reader gets a deeper understanding of how Rodriguez was able to form strong bonds while living between two cultural worlds.

 Rodriguez's memoir is also filled with love and optimism. Through his own life experiences, he provides a hopeful outlook about his life and how his roots have been able to shape his identity. Rodriguez leaves readers with the hope that their own journeys will will bring them joy and adventure.

The tone of Rodriguez's memoir is very comforting. It can be easy to get lost in the nostalgia of his childhood and the beauty of his family’s stories. There is a strong theme of forgiveness and hope throughout the book which creates a sense of peace. Rodriguez captures the essence of his culture and upbringing so that readers can connect to each new discovery.
