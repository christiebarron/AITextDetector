

In Winter Hibiscus, the narrator is a refugee from Laos who is struggling to adjust to life in America. The ending suggests that the narrator has been studying hard for an important test; however, the cold winter arrives and makes it difficult to focus on the task at hand. She finds solace in a hibiscus plant that blooms despite the cold weather, provides her with hope that spring will come and she will have the opportunity to take the test. The last line emphasizes the determination of the narrator and her belief that spring will come and she will find the strength to take the test and prove herself.
