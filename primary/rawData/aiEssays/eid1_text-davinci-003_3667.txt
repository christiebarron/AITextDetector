

Dear Editor,

As technology continues to evolve, computers are becoming increasingly commonplace in our lives. While the advantages of having a computer are clear, there is a growing concern about the potential negative effects this technology may have on people.

First and foremost, there is the issue of sedentary behaviour. Studies have shown that spending too much time in front of a computer can lead to physical inactivity, which can be detrimental to one’s overall health. Those who spend too much time on their computers may be more likely to suffer from obesity, diabetes, and cardiovascular disease.

It is also believed that heavy computer usage can influence our cognitive abilities. Research has suggested that extended time spent in front of a computer may impair our problem-solving skills, as well as our ability to think critically and pay attention. Furthermore, addiction to computer games and online gambling can be a serious concern for young people.

These effects can be minimized by setting healthy limits for computer usage. We should ensure that we still engage in physical activities, and make time for pursuits that stimulate our minds in different ways. Parents should also be more cognizant of their children’s computer usage, to avoid any significant adverse health effects.

It is necessary that we recognize the potential risks of computers, and take the necessary steps to minimize them in order to enjoy the many benefits of this technology.
