

Dear Editor, 

As computers become increasingly integrated into our daily lives, it’s worth considering the impact that they can have on our physical and mental health. On the one hand, computers can be extremely useful when used properly—they facilitate communication, streamline work, and provide easy access to a wealth of information. However, it’s imperative that we acknowledge the potential dangers of computer overuse. 

Overexposure to screens can have a range of negative effects on our overall wellbeing, leading to problems such as eye strain, headaches, fatigue, and insomnia. Moreover, the pressures of the online world can induce a lack of self-esteem and put us in an unhealthy scarcity mindset. 

The current environment which privileges productivity over mental health must change. We must be mindful of the amount of time we spend on computer-based activities, making sure to take regular breaks and prioritize meaningful face-to-face interactions with friends and family over digital ones. We should also understand that we don't need to be available to respond to emails and messages 24/7; unplugging from technology every once in a while is crucial for keeping us physically and mentally healthy.
