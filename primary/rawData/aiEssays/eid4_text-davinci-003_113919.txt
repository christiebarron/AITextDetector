

This ending reflects the main characters emotional journey throughout the poem. The speaker's journey of self-confidence is symbolised by the hibiscus, which is fragile and needs protecting through the cold winter while the speaker takes the risk of attempting the test despite their insecurities. At the end of the poem, the speaker is now feeling confident and assured in their self worth. The winter is ending and the hibiscus will be blooming again, showing the speaker's growth in confidence and resilience. By ending with 'when the snows melt and the geese return and this hibiscus is budding, then I will take that test again' the speaker tells readers that no matter the result of the first test, they will take the test again in the future regardless as they have built up their self-confidence to do so.
