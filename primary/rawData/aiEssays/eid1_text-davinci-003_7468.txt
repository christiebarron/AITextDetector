

The ending of Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho is meant to show that hardships faced now can be overcome with time. Though the narrator fails the interview, he finds solace in the hope of spring bringing a new opportunity and a second chance. The return of the geese, the melting snow, and the budding hibiscus all represent a new beginning. The narrator will use the time in between to better himself and be more prepared for the next opportunity. In this way, the ending symbolizes the cycle of life and how perseverance will always bring about a brighter future.
