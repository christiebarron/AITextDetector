

The ending of the poem reflects the narrator's attitude of resilience and optimism towards her journey in life--her struggles to work hard in school and to achieve her goals. The narrator compares the renewed life of the winter hibiscus with her own determination to stay focused on her goal despite facing setback. The world outside and the energy of growth and renewal serves as a source of inspiration for the narrator when she experiences temporary frustration or failure. The conclusion of the poem gives a message of hope and perseverance in the face of uncertainty and difficulty. The narrator yearns to take the test again in the spring, showing that she's prepared to take on the challenge again. The ending also represents a cyclical nature to life--that failure and difficulty are not the end, but a point from which to start over and keep on striving.
