

The story's protagonist is a young immigrant student who moves to the United States from Singapore with her mother. They struggle to adjust to their new life, and the protagonist experiences feelings of isolation and hopelessness. At the story's end, the protagonist reflects on the struggles she has faced, including the difficult English language test she failed. She finds hope in the prospect of leaving it all behind when the snows melt and the geese return - bringing with them the reminder that winter is only temporary. The protagonist finds hope in the idea that she will have a chance to take the test again in the spring, when the hibiscus plant buds. This idea of hope gives her strength and courage to continue fighting until that time.
