

The ending of "Winter Hibiscus" by Minfong Ho helps to emphasize the idea that hard work and dedication pays off. While the narrator was feeling anxious and uncertain about her upcoming college entrance exams, she takes a moment to pause and remember the resilience and determination of her grandmother, who planted hibiscus flowers that could survive the winter despite the harsh conditions. The narrator chooses to take the example of her grandmother's courage and renews her dedication to her studies, recognizing that the all her hard work will most definitely bear fruit. The narrator then affirms her dedication to her studies by stating that she will take the test again when the hibiscus buds appear in the spring, which symbolizes that despite all adversity she will strive for excellence and persevere until she prevails.
