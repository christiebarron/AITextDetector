

The ending of the poem reflects the theme of hope and renewal that Minfong Ho has developed throughout the poem. By ending with the hopeful image of the hibiscus budding and renewing, the author reinforces her suggestion that anything is possible and that one can persevere and succeed despite of any obstacles or hardships. The phrase 'When they come back in the spring' and 'when the snows melt and the geese return' is also a metaphor for spring, a season of new beginnings and fresh starts. The imagery of the poem conveys the resilience of the speaker and their determination to try again and face whatever hardships may come. The hopeful image of the hibiscus budding serves as a reminder that anything is possible and that one can overcome any obstacles and difficulties in order to reach the success that they desire.
