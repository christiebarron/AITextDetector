Other building features included the former Central Office Building, under construction for the last 10 years, and the historic Woodrow Wilson Building on West 33rd Street in the East Village.

In an interview with The New York Times in 1999, Ritchey said the idea of using unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) had been in development for 30 years.

"It's been around 30 years, I think, but it was a thought long time ago when the air space was so small that the idea of flying them over the city actually actually took off. And now they seem very interested in building this sort of thing," he argued

"There's been no discussion about this until quite recently, so that's what we're doing now," Ritchey went on.

The question of UAVs was raised by New York Times reporters Richard Cohen and Alan Abramowitz in March 2000, who claimed that UAVs might be able to fly over the city.

But then a federal law was created and the UAV ban moved forward. It also changed the law to let police be allowed to fly unmanned aircraft over open park spaces.
