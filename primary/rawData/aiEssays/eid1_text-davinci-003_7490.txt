

The ending of Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho serves as a metaphorical promise to the protagonist that a challenging time will - eventually - pass. The metaphor of the hibiscus flower provides a concrete example of how hard times can - and will - pass. The ending line conveys the notion of hope, as the protagonist is looking forward to a time when things will be better. It also serves to reinforce the idea of persistence and resilience, as the protagonist is again willing to face the music and take the test once more.
