

In recent years, the topic of censorship in libraries has become an increasingly debated subject. On one side of the debate, there are those who believe that certain materials, such as books, music, movies, and magazines, should be removed from the shelves if they are found to be offensive. On the other side of the debate, there are those who believe that censorship should not be practiced in libraries, as it goes against the value of free speech and expression.

It is essential to maintain the free exchange of ideas and to protect the right of individuals to make their own decisions regarding their beliefs and values; therefore, censorship should not be required or practiced in libraries. Libraries should be respectful of all beliefs and be a safe place to explore a variety of topics and perspectives without fear of judgment or censorship.

By censoring library materials, individuals are being denied access to information and knowledge. Libraries should be seen as a safe environment for individuals of all ages to further explore topics and gain insight on matters of current interest and of historical importance. Censoring the source material limits the opportunity of an individual to gain an understanding of certain topics they may otherwise not be exposed to or experience otherwise.

Moreover, censorship should not be used to limit potential sources of entertainment in an expansive library. Individuals should have the freedom to express themselves through various forms of entertainment, which provide an opportunity to explore different topics, points of view, and can even provide a platform to foster creativity.
