

The ending of the story relates to the protagonist's journey of self-discovery. By the end of the story, she has gained strength and maturity to confront and overcome her fears. The statement about the test is a sign of her newfound confidence. She is saying that even if she does not pass the test again, she is still comfortable with who she is and it is no longer a source of frustration or fear for her. The imagery of returning geese, melting snow, and the budding hibiscus is symbolic of the season of renewal, a metaphor for the process of self-growth the protagonist has undergone.
