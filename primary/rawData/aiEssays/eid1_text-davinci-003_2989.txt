

Dear Editor,

I am writing to share an important opinion of mine that concerns the effects computers have on people. Computers have had a tremendous impact on our everyday lives, from the way we communicate to the way we spend our free time. 

On the one hand, computers are an invaluable resource in teaching, learning, and research. They allow us to quickly access information that would otherwise be impossible to find. They can be used to create beautiful works of art, photographs, and videos. Computers save people time, streamline processes, and enable us to interact with distant colleagues or family members. 

On the other hand, too much reliance on computers can cloud our minds and lead to distraction. We can become so dependent on technology that we miss out on key interpersonal experiences. Without face-to-face interactions, we start to lose important skills such as listening and collaborating. Furthermore, some people become overwhelmed by the amount of information that is available online.
