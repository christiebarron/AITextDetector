

The narrator of Winter Hibiscus ends the story with hope that the winter hibiscus can come back to life in spring. By associating the hibiscus with a forthcoming difficult exam, the narrator is optimistic that, if the hibiscus can survive and bloom, so will she. In addition, this statement ties in with the theme of renewal present throughout the story. The hope that everyone in the story will be able to come together and bounce back from the difficulties they have faced binds the ending into a full circle, suggesting that the narrator has learned and grown from the experience.
