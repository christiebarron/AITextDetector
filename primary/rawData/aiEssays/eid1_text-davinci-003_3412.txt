

Dear Editor,

Recently, there has been a lot of discussion about the effects of computers on people. As a concerned citizen, I would like to express my opinion on this matter.

Computers can undoubtedly be a force of good in people’s lives. They can be used to help us become more connected with others, help us with our work, and even help us learn in school. However, it is important to consider the potential drawbacks of computers when evaluating their effect on people. From my experience, computers can make us more isolated and can lead to information overload.

In today’s world, it seems like everyone is constantly plugged in to one device or another. We can be “connected” to each other without ever having face-to-face interactions. Often, we find ourselves in a world of virtual relationships that lack the depth and understanding that come with genuine physical connections.
