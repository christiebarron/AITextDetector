Computers have many effects on people. Some are to encourage, some will be detrimental to it. Others cause it. These effects can be hard to observe objectively.

Research by the University of Washington suggests we need to take into account what we are feeling as we understand, and what happens during these moments of heightened emotions. The researchers say when we take into account how long the emotions are going to be felt, we may think we gain more control over them.

The researchers report in the Journal of Personality and Social Psychology that they now think about what might be called the "disruption effect." They hypothesize that this disruption of our thoughts and behaviors is how our feelings can turn out to be more negative. It can be hard to see how negative thoughts can go on for hours or days at a time, or during the days when we are feeling calm.
