

The last lines of Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho serve to emphasize the narrator's confidence in their future. The return of the geese and the flowering of the hibiscus symbolize renewal and growth, suggesting that despite the narrator's struggles in the present, they will be able to overcome them. By choosing to retake the test in the spring, the narrator is not only showing their fearlessness in the face of adversity, but also expressing optimism in the possibility of future success. The imagery of renewal symbolizes the narrator's hope for a better future, and also their belief in their own potential.
