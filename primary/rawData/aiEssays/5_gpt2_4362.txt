The mood created in the memoir 'From Home is' was in tune with an earlier era of British-speaking post-New Orleans: "It is, in other words, a very contemporary way of being. As with the rest of New York, a sort of political identity has become less and less prominent."

New Orleans as a city, particularly in the last few years, has become a point of departure to American culture from the post-Civil War period. And just to make things more more interesting, the city has long been an influence on the political and economic life of the nation.
