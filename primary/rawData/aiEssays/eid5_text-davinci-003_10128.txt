

Narciso Rodriguez’s memoir, From Home: The Blueprints of Our Lives, is a delightful journey through his childhood and adolescence up to his place in the fashion industry. The mood created in the memoir is one of nostalgia, optimism, and contentment. 

Through vivid descriptions and anecdotes, Rodriguez crafts a world where he and his family are truly happy and content with life. From the opening pages of Rodriguez’s childhood memories in the quiet suburbs of New Jersey to the bustling streets of New York City, the memoir takes us back to a time when things seemed simpler and more wholesome. Rodriguez’s romanticized view of his adolescence and his family’s close-knit culture creates an atmosphere of nostalgia, coupled with the optimism of what the future holds. 

As Rodriguez captures both the joy and the struggles of being a Latino in the US in the 1970s and 80s, hopefulness emerges in his story. In his journey from the suburban sprawl of New Jersey to the boisterous energy of Greenwich Village, this emerging optimism is palpable. Through his hard work and dedication, Rodriguez proves that success is found in embracing one’s cultural heritage and striving to do the best work possible.
