Over the past few decades, computers have become an integral part of our daily lives. While the rise of computers has brought countless advantages, there are several potential negative effects to consider.

When computers are used for work, they can increase productivity and enhance decision-making in businesses. However, there is some evidence that prolonged exposure to computers can lead to eye strain, fatigue, stress, and lower back pain. Prolonged sitting in front of a computer can also have a negative impact on our health, resulting in reduced physical activity.

Additionally, computers can distract us from our real-world commitments. Enmeshed in a digital world, we can spend too much time on our computers and neglect family and important life assignments. This can lead to mental burnout, which has been linked to depression, anxiety, and depression-related illnesses.

Finally, since computers are powerful tools that can be used to spread false or malicious information, it can lead to the perception of a false reality. This phenomenon can result in us making decisions based on false information and lead to dangerous consequences.

Overall, computers can have a negative effect on the well-being of individuals and society. Therefore, it is important to develop strategies that help us to create a better balance between computers and our daily lives. We must also remain vigilant in fact-checking information and be aware of false reality.
