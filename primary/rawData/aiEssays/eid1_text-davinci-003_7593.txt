

The ending of Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho serves to give the reader a sense of hope. It suggests that no matter what you are facing today, you can try again tomorrow. The hibiscus flower in the poem is a symbol of growth and renewal, which serves to give the reader hope that there is still a chance to achieve success. By ending with the protagonist's resolve to take the test again in the spring, it shows that there is always opportunity for a fresh start, and that there is hope in continuing to strive onward.
