

The ending of "Winter Hibiscus" by Minfong Ho serves to give hope to the narrator. The narrator has been struggling with a difficult test and is feeling overwhelmed. The narrator takes solace in the fact that the hibiscus will bloom in the spring, and with the return of the geese comes a greater chance of success in his studies. This serves to give the narrator hope that, by the spring, he will be able to take the test again and be successful.
