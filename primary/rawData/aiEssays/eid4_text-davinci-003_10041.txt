

The ending line of Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho reflects the protagonist's growth in overcoming her fear of failure in school and being able to take control of her own life. The line also signifies the symbolism of the hibiscus flower, which is a metaphor for the protagonist's need to break out of her shell and blossom. The line is also a reminder of the protagonist's courageous spirit and her newfound strength and perseverance. It signifies her determination to work hard and overcome any obstacle life may present.
