

The narrator of Winter Hibiscus is a young student from a rural Cambodian village, and he is struggling with his studies and a difficult English exam. At the end of the story, the narrator is still facing the English test and he is hoping that the winter hibiscus will bring him luck on the test. He believes that if he sees the hibiscus flower in the spring, then he will have the luck and courage to pass the test. The final line of the story serves as a reminder to himself: when the winter snows have melted and the spring brings the hibiscus, he must take the test again. This line serves as a bit of hope and encouragement for the narrator as he prepares to face his challenges.
