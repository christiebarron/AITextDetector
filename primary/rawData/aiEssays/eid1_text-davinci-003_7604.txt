

The speaker in Minfong Ho's poem Winter Hibiscus is reflecting on a difficult experience, in which they fear they may have failed a test they took earlier. The poem ends with the speaker expressing hope that, when the snows melt and the geese return in the spring, they will get the chance to take the test again. This passage reinforces the hope and optimism that the speaker feels despite their prior failures and struggles, and it encourages readers to keep striving towards their goals, even in challenging times.
