

This ending serves as a symbol of hope and perseverance for the protagonist. The winter hibiscus blooms in the cold months and represents the hard, difficult times in life. By comparing the protagonist to the hibiscus, Ho is suggesting that despite the challenges and setbacks in life, if they invest in themselves and persist, they can eventually see growth. The protagonist's situation in the story is that she plans to try and take a college entrance exam again, presumably after having failed it the first time. This metaphor serves as a reminder that if she works hard enough, she can still have a successful future.
