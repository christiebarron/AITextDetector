The narrator is reflecting on her difficult academic experience and is reminded of her strength and resilience to persevere and succeed in the face of adversity. The narrator is cognizant of the changes that occur on a regular basis in nature, in contrast to her own struggles, and she applies the cycle of nature to her own life. By saying, “When they come back in the spring, when the snows melt and the geese return and this hibiscus is budding, then I will take that test again,” the author is implying that the narrator will again persevere no matter the obstacles that arise and be successful in her academic journey. The optimism and hope present in the ending of the poem serves as a reminder to readers that no matter what they face, to be resilient and persevere.
