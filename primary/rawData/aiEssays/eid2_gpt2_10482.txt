In 2014, as the debate raged inside the federal judiciary over an attempt to limit the free expression of religious publications in the country, the Supreme Court considered a landmark ruling that struck down the freedom of the press.

But the ruling, which was upheld by the high court only eight years later, was an unwelcome end to a decade of activism and political activism in the courts. For some religious organizations, the Supreme Court's decision to strike down an attempt to limit freedoms of speech and expression is nothing short of a "wasteful" victory as a result.
