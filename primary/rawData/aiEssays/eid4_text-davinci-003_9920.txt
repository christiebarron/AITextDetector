

The author wants to end the poem with a message of hope. The imagery of spring and the return of the geese imply that the narrator will have the chance to retake the test and have a fresh start with a renewed sense of optimism. The narrator has been through a difficult period in their life which the hibiscus symbolizes, and the promise of a new beginning in the spring is what gives the narrator hope and courage to move on from their struggles. This hopeful ending contributes to the overall optimistic tone of the poem.
