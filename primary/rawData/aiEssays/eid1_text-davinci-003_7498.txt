

The narrator in the story is Uma, a young girl from Thailand who has just moved to the United States. By ending the story with the line, "When they come back in the spring, when the snows melt and the geese return and this hibiscus is budding, then I will take that test again," Minfong Ho is showing Uma's determination and optimism. Although Uma is in a new and unfamiliar country, she is determined to succeed and prove her worth. She is looking to the future with hope and is confident that everything will eventually work out. The hibiscus plant provides a symbol of her resilience and fortitude. It is her reminder that even if things are difficult now, she can draw strength from the memory of her homeland. Uma knows that when the spring comes, she can pick herself up, attempt the test again, and succeed.
