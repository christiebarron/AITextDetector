Some of the arguments made by some libertarians in his comments are similar to those leveled at liberal writers by William F. Buckley when he challenged the way he conducted himself and the way he interpreted the United States Constitution.

The most significant example was Buckley's defense of Richard Nixon's "enabling, restraining, and de facto deregulating efforts to undermine and defame the Constitution" (Preston, 1993).
