

The setting of ‘Rough Road Ahead’ by Joe Kurmaski has a huge impact on the cyclist in the story. The setting consists of a rough and hilly mountain terrain, populated by sparsely spread trees and few signs of human life. This is an environment the cyclist is not familiar with. The terrain is difficult to navigate and provides the cyclist with a physical challenge that tests his limits and pushes him to persevere despite difficulties and frustrations.

The cyclist is motivated to push onwards despite the difficulties of the terrain partly because environment provides a sense of challenge and opportunity. The fact that he is a stranger to these hills allows him to feel out of place and to develop a deeper appreciation for nature and his own physical abilities. Furthermore, being in a quieter, isolated environment, away from the hustle and bustle of city life, allows the cyclist to reach a higher level of mental clarity. 

The cyclist is temporarily free from the worries of daily life because of the isolated and quiet setting and can feel as if he is taking a journey, not just a physical one but also an emotional and intellectual one as he pushes himself and pushes on through the hard terrain.
