

To the Editor:

As computer technology evolves rapidly, I think it is necessary to consider its potential risks and benefits. In my opinion, computers have a significant and often underestimated effect on people.

On one hand, computers provide numerous advantages that can't be overlooked. Without them, it would be impossible to find solutions to some of the world's most pressing problems. Computers are also great tools for communication, allowing us to stay connected with people who live nearby and around the world.

On the other hand, computers also have the power to disrupt our lives. People may become withdrawn and isolated by spending too much time in front of a computer screen. Our physical and mental health can suffer if we don't take appropriate breaks and exercise regularly.

For these reasons, I urge all readers to consider the impact of computers on themselves and others. We should use computers responsibly and view them as a tool that is meant to enhance our lives, not control them. It is important to remember that technology does not always replace human interaction.
