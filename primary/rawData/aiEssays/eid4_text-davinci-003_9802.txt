

The protagonist of Winter Hibiscus is an immigrant from Thailand who is studying for her US citizenship test. The continual fear of failing the test is a source of anxiety for her. The ending of the novel provides a hopeful resolution. By concluding with the statement that she will take the test again in the spring, it implies that she has enough time to work on the material, and that she is confident that she will pass when she takes it a second time. It suggests that she will find a way to overcome the challenges she is facing. This ending provides a feeling of optimism and hope for the character, illustrating that success is possible if one keeps pursuing their goals.
