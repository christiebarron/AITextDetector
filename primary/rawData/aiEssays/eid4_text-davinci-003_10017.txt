

The ending of Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho is a reminder of the narrator's goal to go to college and take her test again. The imagery of the spring, melting snow, and returning geese is used to represent a moment of hope, joy, and new beginnings. The narrator is emphasizing that even when times seem dark and challenging, there is always the potential of new opportunities that come with the new season. She is emphasizing her determination to reach her goals and is hopeful that when the spring comes and the hibiscus returns, then she will have the chance to take her test again.
