

The final line of Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho serves to symbolize hope and resilience in the face of difficulty. The narrator has been struggling in school and is feeling overwhelmed by the pressure of the upcoming tests. However, by ending with the narrator expressing a desire to try again when the hibiscus blooms and the geese return in the spring, Minfong Ho encourages the reader to be hopeful and confident in the face of difficulties. The line implies that the narrator will have the strength and resilience to succeed, even if they have been struggling up until that point. In this way, the final line of Winter Hibiscus serves to empower the reader and showcase the importance of resilience and perseverance in overcoming life's challenges.
