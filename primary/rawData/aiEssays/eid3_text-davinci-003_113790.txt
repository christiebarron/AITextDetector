

In “Rough Road Ahead” by Joe Kurmaski, the cyclist is affected by several features of the setting. Foremost, the weather conditions that the cyclist encounters have a significant influence on his ride. The cyclists finds himself encountering heavy rain and strong winds that make the conditions of the ride much more difficult. In addition to the rain, strong headwinds further impede progress, making it more difficult to make any headway during the ride.

A second feature of the setting that affects the cyclist is the topography of the land over which he is riding. Initially he sets off on a flat road that seems to be calm but soon finds himself climbing up steep hills, crawling his way to the top. Even though he struggles against the steep inclines he continues to fight on, determined to reach his destination.

The final feature of the setting that affects the cyclist is the darkness of night that has begun to settle in around him. As the dusk falls, the cyclist is enveloped in darkness and he is forced to press on without the assistance of light. 

In summary, the features of the setting in “Rough Road Ahead” by Joe Kurmaski have a significant influence on the cyclist’s ride. The weather, topography and darkness all had profound affects on the cyclist’s journey, making it much more difficult than it otherwise would have been.
