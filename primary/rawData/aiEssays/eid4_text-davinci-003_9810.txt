

The ending of Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho is symbolic in that it serves to illustrate the cyclical nature of life and the protagonist's decision to take control of his life by taking control of his education. The protagonist has determined to take the not-readily-available test in the spring so that he can continue his studies. By ending with this promise, the author implies that, despite the challenges and hardships the protagonist faces, he will ultimately overcome them and continue to strive forward. In this way, the author encourages readers to have hope for the future and take control of their destiny.
