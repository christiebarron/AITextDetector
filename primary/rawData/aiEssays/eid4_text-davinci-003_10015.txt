

The author uses this ending to emphasize the importance of perseverance and hope in the face of adversity. The winter hibiscus serves as a metaphor for the protagonist's struggles. Despite the cold winter, and her struggles to pass a test, the protagonist has hope that she will be able to overcome the difficulties she faces and succeed in the end. The author's use of nature conveys the message that despite the bleakness of winter, spring will come and with it, the promise of renewal and growth. In this ending, the protagonist is determined to succeed, as evidenced by her intention to take the test again in the spring. The ending serves as a reminder that with dedication and perseverance, we can triumph over our obstacles and enjoy the promise of a new beginning.
