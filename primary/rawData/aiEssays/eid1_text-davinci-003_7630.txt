

Minfong Ho ends the poem with the hibiscus as a metaphor for the main character, Leng. The hibiscus is a reminder that despite the cold weather of winter, life will continue in the spring. Spring symbolizes new hope and possibility, and the return of the geese represent the journey of life. By ending the poem with Leng taking the test again when the hibiscus blooms in the spring, Minfong Ho provides readers a positive, hopeful outlook of the future, regardless of how dark and bleak one's current circumstance may be. Ultimately, Minfong Ho uses this ending to provide readers with hope and reassurance that there is light at the end of the tunnel, regardless of obstacles or hardships that one may encounter.
