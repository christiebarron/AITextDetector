

The ending of Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho causes the reader to look towards a hopeful future. The image of the hibiscus flower budding in the spring serves as a metaphor for the main character's hope that her hard work throughout winter will pay off in the end. The line suggests that she will try the test again when the season of winter passes, as a sign of revival and renewed hope. It also shows that she remains determined to succeed despite the challenges of winter.
