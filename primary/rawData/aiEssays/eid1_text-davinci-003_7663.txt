

The ending of the poem implies that the speaker wants to gain a better insight into the beauty of the winter hibiscus through a retaking of a past test. By concluding the poem with these lines, the speaker implies that the hibiscus's presence will be a reminder of the importance of following their dreams and striving to do better. It also indicates that, even though the hibiscus is expected to eventually bloom, the present season of winter will eventually turn to spring and things will eventually become brighter, more hopeful, and more prosperous. The speaker intends to use the winter hibiscus as a symbol of resilience and hope in times of struggle.
