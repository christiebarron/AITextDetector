

The ending of Winter Hibiscus serves as a reminder that although the protagonist does not pass his test, there will be opportunities for another chance later on. The passage emphasizes the idea of hope for the future and the necessity of perseverance when faced with obstacles. The imagery of the melting snow, returning geese, and budding hibiscus conveys a sense of renewal and provides optimism for the protagonist’s future try at the test. At the same time, the protagonist’s determination to try again despite his failure expresses an admirable work ethic, further reinforcing the idea of hope in the face of setbacks.
