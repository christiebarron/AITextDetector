

The ending of Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho provides a resolution to the narrator's struggle with his immigrant identity. The narrator is reminded of the importance of understanding his roots and continues to strive towards developing a balanced sense of identity with the help of his grandmother. By reminding the narrator of the importance of balance between his two cultural identities, the narrator realizes the importance of his heritage. The ending of the story serves as a reminder of this importance, by indicating that the narrator will attempt to connect with his identity again in the spring when the snow melts and the geese return. This implies that the narrator will make an effort to start anew and try to find that balance between the two cultural identities again. This ending provides a sense of hope and optimism for the narrator, that he will be able to seek that balanced identity even after the challenges he has faced in the story.
