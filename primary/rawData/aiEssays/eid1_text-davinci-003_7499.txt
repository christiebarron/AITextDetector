

The speaker in this poem is expressing their desire to take a test in the spring, when there is hope for a new beginning. The winter hibiscus symbolizes the speaker's struggles with the test and the strength and courage they possess in facing this challenge. The poem ends with an expression of hope for the spring, when the snow has melted, the geese have returned, and the hibiscus is starting to show signs of life, which is a sign of a new beginning and a reminder for the speaker to take the test they fear again. The imagery of the hibiscus blooming in the spring serves as a symbol of hope and renewal, and as a reminder to persevere in their academic pursuits.
