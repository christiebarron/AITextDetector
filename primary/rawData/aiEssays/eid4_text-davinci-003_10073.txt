

Minfong Ho ends Winter Hibiscus with this sentence to demonstrate the resilience and confidence of the young protagonist. The phrase reinforces the idea that the protagonist has grown and learned from her experiences throughout her journey and now she is willing to face whatever challenge lies ahead with stoicism and fortitude. The mention of the geese and snow melting with the hibiscus symbolizes the end of winter and her previous struggles. The words "when the snows melt and the geese return and this hibiscus is budding," suggest hope for the future and the strength of character the protagonist has gained. The test she refers to could be interpreted as a challenge that stands in the way of her progress forward, which she will face with renewed self-confidence after the long winter.
