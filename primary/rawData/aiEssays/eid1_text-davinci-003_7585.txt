

The ending of Winter Hibiscus by Minfong Ho reflects the protagonist's hope and determination for a better future. The protagonist is a young refugee in Chinatown who has been living in fear and uncertainty for some time. Through the story, the protagonist has been mentally preparing for a test that will decide his future, but due to the difficult circumstances of his life, he is unable to take the test. By ending the story with the protagonist's contemplation of what will happen when spring comes and the tests come around again, the author conveys the protagonist's hope and determination for a better future. The hibiscus is a symbol of hope and resilience that will blossom when the snows melt and the geese return, and it serves as a reminder of the protagonist's tenacity and optimism.
