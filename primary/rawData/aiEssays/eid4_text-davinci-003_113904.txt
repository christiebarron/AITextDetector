

At the end of the story, the protagonist has been going through a difficult time, struggling to make sense of her grandfather's stories and the meaning of his experiences. The protagonist has just failed a test, and the narrator ends with the characters coming full circle by looking to the future, when the geese return and the hibiscus begins to bud again, as a symbol of hope that the protagonist will have a new chance to take the test and succeed. The ending reflects the idea of overcoming difficulties through resilience, taking heart from the eventual arrival of spring and the chance of new beginnings.
