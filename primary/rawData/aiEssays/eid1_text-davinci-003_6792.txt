

Dear [Editor of Newspaper],

I am writing this letter to share my opinion about the effects that computers have on people in our society. I firmly believe that while computers can be a useful tool in many aspects of life, they should be used in moderation and appropriate safeguards should be put into place to protect users.

One of the primary ways that computers can have a negative effect on people is that they can be a source of distraction. From e-mails and social media notifications to a simple browser window full of interesting websites, it’s easy to become sidetracked when you’re sitting in front of a computer all day. Even a few minutes here and there can add up over the course of the day, leaving you with less time and energy for the important things in life like family, friends, and hobbies.

Another concern is how computers can easily be used to access and spread harmful content, both intentionally and unintentionally. Aside from the obvious malicious criminal behavior associated with these activities, people can unintentionally spread false or misleading information if they rely too heavily on search engine results or social media sources that are not credible.

Finally, there is the risk of computer addiction, specially among children. What starts out as simple curiosity can turn into a full-blown addiction if unchecked. It is important for parents to be aware of this risk and to monitor the amount of time their children spend in front of the computer.
