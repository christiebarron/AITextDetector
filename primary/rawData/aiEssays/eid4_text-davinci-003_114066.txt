

Minfong Ho ends Winter Hibiscus with this line to convey the idea of resilience and hope in the face of challenging circumstances. The promise of the return of the geese, the melting of the snow, and the budding of the hibiscus all contribute to a sense of optimism and anticipate a new start, which is mirrored in the protagonist's plan to take the English test again in the spring. The promise of spring provides a sense of hope and optimism that things will eventually get better, even if it doesn't seem possible right now.
