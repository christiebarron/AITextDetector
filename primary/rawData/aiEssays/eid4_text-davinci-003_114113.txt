

Minfong Ho ends Winter Hibiscus with this hopeful line to symbolize determination and resilience in the face of adversity. The speaker in the poem is reflecting on the struggles of her life and learning that there are times to give up and other times in which to keep persevering, come what may. The image of the hibiscus symbolizes the speaker's growth and journey, and the speaker reflects that if the hibiscus can survive the hardships of winter, she can too. By ending the poem with the hope that hardship may eventually lead to success and triumph, Minfong Ho is able to connote the larger message of the poem: that although hardships may be inevitable and formidable, they can be overcome through determination and strength of spirit.
