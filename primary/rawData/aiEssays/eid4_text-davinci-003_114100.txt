

The author is conveying the metaphor of "resurrection" with her imagery of the hibiscus blooming in the spring after winter passes. The analogy of the hibiscus blooming is extended to the protagonist, who plans to take the test again in the spring. The phrase "when the snows melt and the geese return and this hibiscus is budding" help create a hopeful and optimistic tone to the poem, suggesting that the protagonist won't give up and will try and take the test once again even if she failed it before. The ending phrase also alludes to the idea of rebirth and signifies that the protagonist will start the process of growing and learning from her mistakes. In this way, the author's ending conveys the message of hope, resilience, and growth.
