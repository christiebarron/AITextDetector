

Narciso Rodriguez’s memoir ‘From Home: The Blueprints of Our Lives' is a captivating, heartfelt account of his rise to fame as a world-renowned fashion designer, combined with the vivid recollections of his childhood, growing up in the small town of Dover, New Jersey. It’s an exploration of his identity and a journey towards redemption, as Rodriguez begins to take control of his life.

Throughout the memoir, Rodriguez paints a vivid picture of his childhood, growing up under the watchful eyes of his over-protective Cuban parents in New Jersey. He poignantly captures moments of both fear and love, leaving the reader with a deep sense of pathos. From his childhood fear of the “monster” under his father’s bed, to the safety of his mother’s cooking, there is a real sense of nostalgia and fondness that will have readers feeling a real emotional connection to the memoir. 

As the memoir progresses, Rodriguez’s skillful writing paints an image of a hardworking young Rodriguez, determinedly carving his own path in life. It’s a sense of hope and reassurance that any obstacle can be overcome, regardless of your upbringing or background. His determination and love of art and fashion flow through every page, and are a clear homage to his parents, who taught him the importance of hard work and resilience.
